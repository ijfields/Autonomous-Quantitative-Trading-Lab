## What This Guide Covers

How to engineer consumer app onboarding using the "magic moment" framework, reduce cognitive load with "mental calories," and implement 6x super-user monetization — based on the Autopilot app ($18.5M ARR, 300–400% YoY growth).

---

## Prerequisites

- A consumer app or SaaS product with a clear core value proposition
- Ability to A/B test onboarding flows
- Analytics to track conversion at each onboarding step

---

## Step 1: Identify Your Magic Moment

The magic moment is the **single action** that makes a user feel the core value of your product.

1. Ask: "What is the ONE thing I want users to experience?"
2. For Autopilot: executing a real stock trade in the user's brokerage account
3. Test: Have people go through onboarding — if they don't say "Wow" unprompted, it's not strong enough
4. **Rule:** "If you have to catch two rabbits, you catch none" — focus on ONE moment

---

## Step 2: Deliver Magic Moment BEFORE Paywall

The most critical A/B test Autopilot ran:

| Order | Conversion Impact |
|-------|-------------------|
| Magic moment → then paywall | **Baseline (best)** |
| Paywall → then magic moment | **70% worse** |

1. Let users experience core value first
2. Create urgency AFTER value delivery (e.g., "You now own these stocks — pay to keep getting trades")
3. Users who've felt the value are psychologically committed

---

## Step 3: Minimize Mental Calories Per Screen

"Mental calories" = cognitive load required at each onboarding step.

**Reduce mental calories:**
- **Detect context:** Use iOS app detection to show only brokerages the user has installed (not a full list)
- **Limit choices:** Show 4 options max (showing hundreds = 70% less likely to decide)
- **Binary questions:** Convert every decision to yes/no when possible
- **"Show more" pattern:** Hide complexity behind an expandable section

**When high cognitive load is acceptable:**
- Screens that involve anxiety (connecting financial accounts) — users are already focused, not distracted
- Only after you've pulled them out of "Instagram browsing" mode with engaging content

---

## Step 4: Use Exclusivity Gates

The $100K income gate screen:

1. Ask a qualifying question: "Will you make more than $100,000 this year?"
2. If "No" → block access, but allow them to go back and change their answer
3. **Result:** 70% higher paid conversion among people who initially said "No" then switched to "Yes"

**Why it works:**
- App feels like an exclusive club for high earners
- People who feel they "shouldn't be there" complain less and value the product more
- Counterintuitively, blocking people INCREASES conversions

---

## Step 5: Time-Box Onboarding

- **Target:** Complete onboarding in ~90 seconds
- **Test:** Try to complete it while distracted (scrolling Instagram, simulating bathroom break)
- **If you can't finish while distracted, it's too long**
- Include enough "dopamine hits" and provocative marketing messages to break through the distraction

---

## Step 6: Implement 6x Super-User Monetization

From StubHub founder Jeff's advice: "80% of revenue comes from 20% of customers."

1. **Audit:** Can your users currently pay more? If they can only subscribe to one plan, they can only decay
2. **Target:** Users should be able to spend up to **6x their base subscription**
3. **Two approaches:**
   - Single premium tier (e.g., $600 if base is $100)
   - Marketplace of add-on units (each $100, super users buy 6+)
4. **Autopilot result:** 12-month revenue retention went from 60–70% to **110%** (net-positive expansion)

---

## Step 7: Hire for Curiosity and Obsession

- **Key interview question:** "What belief do you hold that people around you disagree with?"
- Curious people have multiple answers and keep elaborating
- Look for elite-level performance in ANY domain (D1 sports, competitive music, etc.)
- D1 athletes: can be pushed hard without taking it personally + proven obsession capacity
- **Offer 3-month severance** to maintain a high hiring bar — makes fast firing sustainable

## Key Takeaway

> Don't sell your product first — prove it works first. Deliver the magic moment before asking for money, reduce cognitive load at every step, use exclusivity psychology to increase perceived value, and ensure super-users can spend 6x base price to achieve net-positive revenue retention.
