## KEY TOPICS

### The Myth: Why People Get Wealth-Building Backwards

- The Rockefellers built wealth **before** trusts — their planning was about control and succession, not creating income
- Most Rockefeller wealth was created before the modern income tax existed
- People see the wrapper (trusts, entities) and assume it created the wealth
- Copying the wrapper too early bleeds money, time, and energy

### Gimmick 1: Spendthrift Trust Scheme

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| Set up a non-grantor irrevocable complex discretionary trust | Section 643 does NOT exclude income from tax |
| Sell assets/income property to the trust | It only determines allocation between trust and beneficiaries |
| Allocate capital gains to corpus, avoid current taxation | If income is not distributed, the **trust** pays the tax |
| IRS can't touch it | IRS issued formal guidance in **2023** confirming this was already obvious |

- **Consequence when audited:** Strategy unwound, deductions disallowed, income taxed where it should have been, plus penalties and interest

### Gimmick 2: 508(c)(1)(A) Church/Ministry Scheme

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| Churches are automatically tax-exempt | True, but only for **actual churches** meeting strict IRS requirements |
| Route business income through a "faith-based foundation" | IRS looks at facts: independent governance, legitimate purpose, no private inurement |
| Pay personal expenses from it | First Amendment has never allowed laundering personal income |

- **Consequence when audited:** Exemption denied, income recharacterized, exposure escalates beyond civil penalties into promoter investigations

### The Reality: The Four-Step Order

1. **Build real value** — Grow a business or income stream where you have an actual advantage
2. **Reinvest in high-ROI assets you understand** — For most: reinvest in your own business until it no longer needs every dollar
3. **Shift excess capital into tax-advantaged assets** — Real estate: depreciation, appreciation, 1031 exchanges
4. **Plan at the right time** — Trusts, estate planning, entity optimization are for **protecting and transferring** wealth, not creating it

## KEY TAX CONCEPTS

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Section 643** | Determines income allocation between trust and beneficiaries; does NOT exclude income from tax |
| **508(c)(1)(A)** | Recognition of church tax exemption; not a loophole for personal income |
| **1031 Exchange** | Tax-deferred exchange of real estate; avoids triggering taxable events |
| **Step-up in basis** | Inherited assets receive new cost basis at fair market value at death |
| **~$15M per person** | Current estate tax exemption threshold — don't engage estate planning until near this level |

## ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

1. **Do not copy what rich people have; replicate what they did, in the right order** — wrapper comes last, not first
2. **Avoid internet tax gimmicks** — if it claims to eliminate income tax while you retain control, it's almost certainly fraud
3. **Build value first** — focus energy on real economic value before complex tax structures
4. **Time your planning correctly** — trusts and FLPs only make sense with material assets near the ~$15M estate tax threshold
5. **Beware premature complexity** — locking assets in irrevocable structures too early can forfeit step-up in basis benefit
