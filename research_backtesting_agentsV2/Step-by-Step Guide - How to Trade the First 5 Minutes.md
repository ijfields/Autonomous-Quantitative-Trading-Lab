## What This Guide Covers

A simple, rule-based day trading setup using the first 5-minute candle of the trading day to define a range (the "fence"), then trading a confirmed breakout-and-retest for a 2:1 reward. Works on QQQ or any liquid instrument. Typically done by 10:00-10:30 AM. Taught by a former market maker.

---

## Prerequisites

- Charting platform with 5-minute candles
- QQQ (or any liquid stock/ETF)
- 20-period simple moving average (SMA) on the chart
- Ability to place limit/stop orders

---

## Step 1: Draw the Fence (9:35-9:36 AM ET)

1. Wait for the first 5-minute candle to close (9:30-9:35)
2. Mark the **high** of that candle → this is the **top rail**
3. Mark the **low** of that candle → this is the **bottom rail**
4. That's your fence — two horizontal lines

**What the fence represents:**
- Everything the market sorted out at the open
- Where buyers stepped in (bottom rail)
- Where sellers pushed back (top rail)
- These levels will be tested again

---

## Step 2: Wait for a Close Outside the Fence

1. Watch subsequent 5-minute candles
2. Look for a candle that **closes completely outside** the fence
   - Close above top rail → bullish breakout
   - Close below bottom rail → bearish breakout
3. **Must be a close, not just a wick** — wicks outside that close inside do NOT count
4. This usually happens within 30-60 minutes of the open

---

## Step 3: Wait for the Retest

1. After the breakout candle closes, watch the very next candle(s)
2. Look for a candle that **wicks back into the fence area** then **closes back outside**
3. This retest confirms that the breakout level is now support (for longs) or resistance (for shorts)
4. **Entry:** The close of this retest candle

**Example (long):**
- Price breaks above top rail and closes above it
- Next candle dips back below the top rail (into the fence) but closes back above
- Enter long at the close of that retest candle

---

## Step 4: Place Your Stop-Loss

1. **Rule of thumb:** About 50% into the fence from your entry side
2. **Better approach:** Look for a clear support/resistance level inside the fence
   - For longs: just below visible support (candle wicks, consolidation)
   - For shorts: just above visible resistance
3. If the fence is very large, move the stop closer to a logical level rather than using the full 50%

---

## Step 5: Set Your Profit Target

1. Measure the distance from entry to stop (your risk, or "1R")
2. Place your target at **2x that distance** in the trade direction (2R)
3. That's your take-profit level

**Adjustments based on anchor line distance:**
- **Near the 20 SMA:** Hold for full 2R with patience
- **Far from the 20 SMA:** Be ready to exit at 1.5-2R; snapback is more likely
- **If hairpin turn forms far from 20:** Exit for ~1R

---

## Step 6: Check the Anchor Line (20 SMA)

Before entering, confirm the 20 SMA direction:

| 20 SMA Direction | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| Moving WITH your trade | Full size, full 2R target |
| Flat | Normal trade, proceed |
| Moving AGAINST your trade | Reduce size or skip the trade |

## Common Scenarios

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Breakout but no retest — price just runs | No trade — this is the weakness; accept missed moves |
| Retest wicks in and closes back outside | Enter at the close of this candle |
| Multiple candles inside fence, no breakout | No trade — wait or move on |
| Very large first candle (wide fence) | Adjust stop to nearest S/R inside fence, not full 50% |
| Far from 20 SMA at entry | Reduce profit expectations; quick exit on reversal signs |
| 20 SMA moving against trade | Skip or reduce size significantly |

## Relationship to Opening Power Bar Strategy

- Stephen's primary strategy is the "Opening Power Bar" on a 2-minute chart
- The Fence Bar is a **backup/complement** for days when the Power Bar doesn't fire
- Both share the same philosophy: defined entry, defined stop, defined target
- Power Bar enters at the breakout; Fence Bar enters at the retest — slightly safer, slightly later
