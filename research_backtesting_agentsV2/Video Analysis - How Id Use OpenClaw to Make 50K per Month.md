## Summary

Mert Yerlikaya and his co-founder present a business-model-focused video on how to turn OpenClaw (the open-source AI agent platform) into a revenue-generating consulting business. They emphasize that the money is not in using the tool for yourself but in the gap between the free tool existing and it actually working inside a real business. OpenClaw requires command-line skills, API keys, security hardening, soul files, and agents files -- things that 95% of business owners cannot do themselves.

The video lays out three escalating tiers of the business model. Level 1 is done-for-you OpenClaw setup ($500-$5,000 per client): a 30-minute discovery call, 3-4 hours of setup, and 2 hours per month of maintenance. Level 2 is recurring AI systems that run parts of a business ($monthly retainer): the video details a real case study with a yacht management company called Stewardly, where they built a WhatsApp-based AI inventory agent that reduced 10 hours of manual inventory to under 1 hour per trip, projecting $21K/year in labor savings and $19K in supply savings. Level 3 is the premium tier ($10,000-$20,000 per engagement): full operational audits where you map every workflow, identify what AI can and cannot solve, and build multi-agent systems. The example given is Flight Freun, a youth sports academy where they replaced an old CRM, built WhatsApp automation, a voice agent for multi-language inquiries, and a staff training agent.

Mert is honest about OpenClaw's limitations: security risks with sensitive data access, hallucinations, skills breaking on updates, and agents doing things they were not asked to do. He stresses that the real skill is understanding business problems, not the technology itself.

## Actionable Takeaways

1. Start with one real business conversation: ask a business owner "What takes up most of your time that you wish just happened on its own?" -- their answer is your first client.
2. Begin at Level 1 ($500-$1,000 per client) for done-for-you OpenClaw setups, then raise prices with proof of results.
3. Move to Level 2 (recurring revenue) by building ongoing AI systems that run parts of a client's business, charging monthly for monitoring and maintenance.
4. The premium Level 3 ($10K-$20K) involves full operational audits -- mapping every workflow and designing multi-agent systems.
5. Focus on the consulting skill (understanding what a business actually struggles with) rather than the technical skill -- that is what commands premium pricing.
6. Be transparent about OpenClaw's limitations with clients: set guard rails, build proper soul files, and plan for ongoing monitoring.
