## Summary
Mert Yerlikaya, who along with his co-founder has generated over $600,000 in revenue building AI agent solutions for businesses, outlines a three-tier business model for monetizing OpenClaw. Rather than focusing on the tool itself, Yerlikaya emphasizes the "gap" between the free, open-source technology and what businesses actually need -- setup, configuration, consulting, and ongoing management. He draws parallels to the web development industry, arguing that even though WordPress and website builders are free, the industry is worth tens of billions because businesses pay to close the gap between tool availability and practical implementation.

The three tiers are: (1) done-for-you OpenClaw setup ($500-$5,000 per client), (2) ongoing AI system management with recurring monthly revenue (illustrated by a yacht management company case study saving $21K/year in labor), and (3) full enterprise AI transformation ($10K-$20K per engagement, illustrated by a youth sports academy case study). Yerlikaya is candid about OpenClaw's limitations, including security risks, hallucinations, and the need for ongoing maintenance, and stresses that the real skill is understanding business problems rather than mastering the tool.

## Actionable Takeaways
1. Start by finding one business owner you know and asking: "What takes up most of your time that you wish just happened on its own?"
2. Offer done-for-you OpenClaw setups starting at $500-$1,000 to build a portfolio of results before raising prices.
3. Focus on recurring revenue by building ongoing AI systems that run parts of a client's business, charging monthly.
4. For premium engagements ($10K-$20K), audit entire business operations to find every workflow that consumes human hours, then design multi-agent solutions.
5. Invest in understanding business problems and consulting skills rather than just technical tool mastery -- the consulting skill is what commands premium pricing.
6. Address security, hallucination, and guard-rail concerns proactively when pitching to businesses with sensitive data.
