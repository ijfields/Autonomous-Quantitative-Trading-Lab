## What This Guide Covers

Key frameworks and predictions from Leopold Aschenbrenner's "Situational Awareness" paper on AGI timelines, the intelligence explosion, and business/security implications.

---

## Step 1: Understand the Three Drivers of AI Progress

1. **Physical compute increases** — massive investment in processing infrastructure (measured in gigawatts)
2. **Algorithmic improvements** — changes to models, math, and approaches
3. **Unhobbling** — removing current limitations: adding memory, Chain of Thought reasoning, persistence (goal-directed behavior), agency (ability to act), and multi-model coordination

---

## Step 2: Grasp the AGI Timeline

1. Aschenbrenner predicts AGI by approximately **2027**
2. Based on GPT-2 → GPT-4 trajectory (orders-of-magnitude scaling)
3. Dario Amodei (Anthropic CEO) made similar predictions: half an order of magnitude every six months
4. This is extrapolation of observed trends, not speculation

---

## Step 3: Think Beyond AGI

1. AGI is just a stepping stone to superintelligence
2. Vision: "a small civilization of billions of AGIs"
3. Implications: industrial explosion, dramatic science/technology leaps, decisive military advantages
4. Don't plan for "slightly better GPT-4" — plan for **orders of magnitude** improvement

---

## Step 4: Prepare Your Business Data Now

1. Save all transcripts, conversations, and multimodal data
2. Label and govern all data assets
3. Thousands of AI models will soon process your data for months-equivalent analysis
4. Companies that prepare data now will have decisive advantage when capabilities arrive

---

## Step 5: Consider Security Implications

1. By 2027-2029, AI becomes a national security issue
2. Model weights for AGI/superintelligent systems currently discussed openly
3. Adversarial nations could acquire weights without doing foundational work
4. Any organization handling sensitive data should assess AI security posture

---

## Key Takeaway

> The paper argues that AGI is not a distant possibility but an extrapolation of current trajectories, arriving as soon as 2027. The business imperative is not to wait for capabilities but to prepare data and infrastructure now. The security imperative is that model weights are too valuable to remain unprotected.
