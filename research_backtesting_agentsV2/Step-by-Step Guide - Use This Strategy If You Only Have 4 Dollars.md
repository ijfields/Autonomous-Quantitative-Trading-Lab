## What This Guide Covers

A daily repeatable day trading strategy for ES/MES (S&P 500) futures using a 5-step price action checklist to identify overextended moves into key zones and trade the reversal. No indicators needed — pure structure analysis with 30-60 minutes of active trading per day. Scalable from $50/trade risk (micro contracts) up to $1,500+.

## Step 1: Mark Key Support/Resistance Zones (9:15 AM ET)

1. Open the **15-minute chart** for ES futures 15 minutes before the stock market opens
2. Identify **major swing highs** — draw a box around the zone
3. Identify **major swing lows** — draw a box around the zone
4. Mark any **trend lines** (upward resistance, downward support)
5. Note where the market is opening relative to these zones

**What you're looking for:**
- Areas where price has reacted before (bounced, reversed, stalled)
- Zones where buyers and sellers have historically stepped in
- These give you the "big picture bias" for the day

**Tip:** Don't over-draw. 2-4 zones maximum — you want clarity, not clutter.

---

## Step 2: Wait for an Unhealthy/Overextended Move

1. Watch the market open and develop on the **1-minute chart**
2. Wait for a **fast, extended spike** into one of your marked zones
3. Signs of an unhealthy move:
   - Big candlesticks in one direction
   - Rapid price change over a short period
   - Price has traveled significantly from its starting point
4. **Do NOT predict the move** — let it happen first, then prepare for the reversal

**Key insight:** "When the market makes a big move up, if it reverses, it reverses in a big way." The bigger the spike, the bigger the potential reversal.

---

## Step 3: Confirm the Trend Break (1-Min Chart)

1. While the spike is happening, watch the **swing structure** on the 1-min chart
2. In an uptrend (for a short setup): watch for higher-highs/higher-lows to break
3. **First signal:** A lower low (price breaks below a previous swing low)
4. **Confirmation:** A lower high follows (price bounces but fails to make a new high)
5. This is the inflection point — the trend may be reversing

**Critical rules:**
- Swings must be **multi-candlestick** moves (3+ candles forming a swing)
- Single-candlestick blips do NOT count as swing highs/lows
- Look for legitimate structure changes, not noise

---

## Step 4: Wait for Momentum Confirmation

1. After the lower-high/lower-low structure forms, wait for a **momentum candle**
2. For shorts: a big bearish candlestick breaking the recent low
3. For longs: a big bullish candlestick breaking the recent high
4. This is your trigger — the market is showing commitment to the reversal

**Optional extra confirmation:**
- If the market is choppy, wait for a pullback AFTER the break, then enter on the re-break
- This avoids "bait" moves where the market breaks a level then immediately reverses

---

## Step 5: Enter with Proper Risk-Reward

1. Place a **sell stop order** (shorts) or **buy stop order** (longs) at the break level
2. Set your **stop-loss** above/below the confirming swing:
   - For shorts: stop above the lower-high swing
   - For longs: stop below the higher-low swing
3. Calculate risk-reward:
   - **Target:** Start of the unhealthy move (where price was before the spike began)
   - **Minimum:** 1:2 risk-reward (risk $100, target $200)
   - **Ideal:** 1:3 risk-reward (risk $100, target $300)
4. If R:R doesn't meet minimum 1:2 → **skip the trade**
5. Enter when order triggers

---

## Step 6: Manage the Trade

### Initial Phase (After Entry)
- Hold position with stop at the confirming swing level
- Be patient — expect some initial choppiness (the $5K trade example was down $600-700 immediately)
- Trust the checklist — if the big picture is right, give it room

### Approaching Initial Target
- When price nears the start of the unhealthy move (your initial target zone):
  - Assess if it's a minor or major support/resistance level
  - Minor: hold for a bigger move to the next major level
  - Major: prepare to tighten or exit

### Momentum Phase (Price Accelerating)
- When price starts making big directional candlesticks in your favor:
  - Trail stop to the **previous candlestick's high/low** as each candle closes
  - This locks in profits while allowing the trend to continue
  - Be aware: "This is also an unhealthy move — it will reverse at some point"

### Exit
- Stopped out by trailing stop, OR
- Manually exit at a major support/resistance zone on the 15-min chart, OR
- Hit your limit order at target price

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Entering before trend break confirms | Wait for multi-candlestick lower-high/lower-low structure |
| Counting single-candle blips as swings | Require 3+ candle swing formations |
| Chasing with negative R:R | Always calculate before entering — skip if below 1:2 |
| Getting scared out on initial choppiness | Trust the big picture; let the stop-loss do its job |
| Holding through reversal of momentum move | Trail stop tightly when price accelerates in your favor |
| Trading every potential setup | Only trade at key S/R zones with unhealthy moves — not every reversal |
| Starting with too much risk | Begin with $50/trade on micro contracts; scale slowly |
