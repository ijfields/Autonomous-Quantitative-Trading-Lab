## What This Guide Covers

How to identify and potentially exploit extreme funding rate dislocations on Hyperliquid's HIP3 traditional market instruments.

---

## Step 1: Monitor HIP3 Funding Rates

1. Check Hyperliquid HIP3 for traditional market instruments (Google, Apple, etc.)
2. Look for extreme negative funding rates (e.g., -241% annualized)
3. Negative funding = longs get paid, shorts pay

---

## Step 2: Assess the Carry Trade

| Funding Rate | Signal |
|-------------|--------|
| < -50% annualized | Strong carry opportunity — longs get paid significantly |
| -20% to -50% | Moderate carry — worth monitoring |
| Near 0% | No carry edge |
| > +50% | Shorts get paid — potential short carry trade |

---

## Step 3: Manage Wick Risk

- HIP3 market makers aggressively push prices around
- **Never trade manually** — wicks will stop you out
- Use wider stops or algorithmic wick detection
- Consider the carry income vs. wick-out risk in position sizing

---

## Risk Warning

- Extreme funding rates are **temporary** — they normalize
- HIP3 is newer with lower liquidity than traditional markets
- Wicks can be severe — defined-risk positions only
- Carry income may not compensate for adverse price moves

---

## Key Takeaway

> Extreme funding rates on HIP3 create carry trade opportunities, but the wick risk makes manual trading dangerous. An algo that captures funding while avoiding wicks could have edge.
