## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Ray Drew, an actual SBA lender, debunks the myth that SBA loans require 10% down payment for commercial real estate. He explains that the SBA 7(a) loan program allows 100% financing for owner-occupied commercial buildings — the SBA's SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) does NOT mandate a standard 10% equity injection. Instead, it requires lenders to assess the business's equity position and determine if an injection is needed. Businesses with strong balance sheets (low debt, retained earnings) can qualify for zero down payment. The key factor is balance sheet health.

## KEY TOPICS

### The Myth: 10% Down Payment Required
- Most banks tell borrowers SBA requires 10% down
- This is the **bank's** requirement, NOT the SBA's
- SBA SOP says: "assess the business's equity position and determine if an equity injection is needed and if so how much"
- No mention of standard 10% requirement

### Financing Options Comparison

| Loan Type | Down Payment |
|-----------|-------------|
| Conventional bank | 20% + closing costs |
| SBA 504 | 10% |
| SBA 7(a) (most banks) | 10% (their rule, not SBA's) |
| SBA 7(a) (informed banks) | 0% possible |

### SBA 7(a) Benefits
- Can include expansion capital in the real estate loan
- Finance equipment, inventory, and working capital alongside the building
- All-in-one financing solution
- Build equity instead of paying landlord
- Keep building when you sell the business → passive income as landlord

### The Key Factor: Balance Sheet Health
- **Strong balance sheet indicators:**
  - In business for many years
  - Low debt in the business
  - Don't drain every dollar (retained earnings)
- **Weak balance sheet indicators:**
  - Overly leveraged
  - Relatively new business
  - Treat business like a piggy bank

### Availability
- Not every bank offers 100% financing
- "A couple dozen" banks do, including Ray's
- Number growing as more SBA lenders catch on
