## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Live town hall at New Birth Missionary Baptist Church (Atlanta) organized by Pastor Jamal Bryant, focused on the "TargetFast" boycott of Target Corporation. The event centers on Target's broken DEI commitments made after the 2020 murder of George Floyd — specifically its pledges to spend $2 billion with Black-owned businesses, fund $100 million in scholarships/nonprofits, increase Black workforce representation by 20%, and donate $10 million to social justice causes — all of which Target has walked away from. Features a panel of civil rights leaders, business advocates, and political figures calling for a sustained boycott, redirection of Black consumer spending toward Black-owned businesses, and long-term economic self-determination.

## KEY TOPICS

### Target's Broken DEI Promises (Core Grievance)
- **$2 billion pledge:** Target voluntarily promised in 2020 to spend $2B with Black-owned businesses by 2025
- **$100 million pledge:** Support Black nonprofits and fund scholarships
- **20% workforce increase:** Increase Black representation (per CNBC)
- **$10 million to social justice:** Per Forbes
- CEO Brian Cornell's original statement: George Floyd "could have been one of his employees"
- All commitments reportedly abandoned — "What changed from 2020 to 2025?"
- Key framing: "We didn't ask them to do it. They volunteered."

### Black Consumer Economic Power
- $1.8 trillion in Black consumer spending
- Black people described as "the nation's lead consumers and embarrassingly least producers"
- "Opening a store in the Black community is not an investment in Black communities — it's just another opportunity to make money"

### Public Opinion on DEI (Ron Busby Data)
- 80%+ of Americans think DEI is good
- 70%+ would choose Black products if quality is equivalent
- Black consumers under 40 search for Black products at least once per week
- Black consumers under 35 search for Black businesses daily

### COVID Impact on Black Businesses
- 41% of all Black-owned businesses lost between February–April 2020
- Primary cause: lack of information (not capital or bad business models)
- Drove US Black Chambers' acquisition of media assets (170 Black radio/TV stations)

### Historical Context: Pattern of Broken Promises
- 40 acres and a mule → Section 8 housing and a bus pass
- Black veterans denied GI Bill benefits after fighting in American wars
- Integration vs. assimilation critique: "Integration never happened — they wanted assimilation"
- Assimilation created a "mindset" of preferring white establishments as the measure of equality

### "White Folk's Ice" Parable (Ron Busby)
- Black grandfather delivered ice; by the time he reached Black communities, it had melted (5.5 days vs. 7)
- Created the literal truth that "white folk's ice was colder"
- But a Black man invented refrigeration — making the disparity irrelevant
- Moral: Black businesses now have full capacity; stop asking for discounts, pay full price so businesses can grow

### Supply Chain and Manufacturing Concerns
- Pamela Booker (Coils by Nature founder, products were in Target before DEI):
  - "Everything is made in China and Mexico"
  - With tariffs + COVID + increased demand, Black manufacturers may run out of supply
  - Need domestic manufacturing infrastructure for jars, plastic components, etc.
- Ron Busby: usblackchambers.org has manufacturing grants available now; **Lindustry** described as "largest Black lender in America"

### Education and Cultural Reclamation (David Johns)
- Schools "were not built for our babies to know who they are"
- Black community has "abdicated responsibility" for education by outsourcing to hostile institutions
- Call to build libraries with banned books
- References Bryan Stevenson: "Systems work exactly as designed"
- References Asa Hilliard: "I've never met a child who is Black who is not a genius"

### LGBTQ+ Inclusion (David Johns)
- Self-identifies as "unapologetic member" of the queer community
- References Bayard Rustin (architect of March on Washington)
- "My queerness is African" — cites Sobonfu Some's "The Spirit of Intimacy" on West African "gatekeeper" roles
- Challenges selective acceptance: "Y'all usually only want me and my gifts to be singing in a choir"
- "Unless and until we understand that we got to love on one another — that's how we actually get free"
