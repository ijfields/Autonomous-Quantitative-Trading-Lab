## What This Guide Covers

How to acquire American Express credit cards in the correct order to maximize every welcome bonus, avoid permanently forfeiting rewards, and navigate the 5 critical Amex rules.

---

## Prerequisites

- Credit score of 670+ (for Level 1)
- No current Amex cards (ideal starting point)
- Willingness to wait 12+ months between tiers

---

## Step 1: Apply for a Level 1 Entry Card

1. Check credit score — need ~670+
2. Use Amex "Apply with Confidence" tool (incognito mode) to verify pre-approval
3. Apply for **American Express Blue Cash Everyday** ($0 annual fee)
   - 3% back on groceries, gas, online shopping (up to $6K combined/year)
   - Disney streaming credit included
4. Use the card actively for **minimum 12 months**
5. Pay balance before statement closing date (not just the due date) to report low utilization

---

## Step 2: Apply for Amex Green (Level 2, First Rung)

1. Credit score should be ~700+ by now
2. **CRITICAL:** Get Green BEFORE Gold — the family language rule permanently kills lower-tier bonuses if you skip ahead
3. Apply for **American Express Green Card** ($150/year)
   - 3x points on travel, transit, and dining
   - Welcome offer: 40,000-60,000 Membership Rewards points
4. If pop-up jail appears ("not eligible for welcome offer"), back out — no hard pull occurred
5. Green/Gold/Platinum are "pay-over-time" cards — exempt from the 1-per-5-days / 2-per-90-days speed limits

---

## Step 3: Apply for Amex Gold (Level 2, Crown Jewel)

1. Wait for Green welcome bonus to post
2. Apply for **American Express Gold Card** ($325/year)
   - Hunt for non-public elevated offer: up to 100,000 MR points
   - $524/year in credits vs. $325 fee = net positive $199/year
3. Activate all monthly/quarterly credits:
   - $10/mo dining (GrubHub, Cheesecake Factory, Five Guys)
   - $50/6mo Resy restaurant credit
   - $7/mo Dunkin' auto-loaded
   - $10/mo Uber Cash
   - $100 hotel credit on 2-night stays

---

## Step 4: Apply for Amex Platinum (Level 3)

1. Credit score should be ~740+
2. Apply for **American Express Platinum** ($895/year)
   - Welcome offer: up to 175,000 MR points after $12K spend in 6 months
   - ~$1,790+ in annual credits (even at 50% utilization, you break even)
3. Key credits to activate:
   - $300/6mo hotel credits (Fine Hotels & Resorts or Hotel Collection)
   - $100/quarter Resy dining
   - $25/mo digital entertainment
   - $75/quarter Lululemon
   - $10/mo+ Uber Cash
   - Airline fee credits, Walmart+, Clear Plus
4. Access 1,550+ airport lounges (Centurion, Delta Sky Club, Priority Pass)

---

## The 5 Rules — Quick Reference

| # | Rule | Action |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | **No Hard Pull** (after first card) | Apply freely — even denials leave no credit mark |
| 2 | **Once-Per-Lifetime Bonus** | Each card's bonus given only once, ever |
| 3 | **Family Language** | Green → Gold → Platinum (NEVER skip ahead) |
| 4 | **Speed Limits** | Credit cards: 1/5 days, 2/90 days. Pay-over-time cards: exempt |
| 5 | **Pop-Up Jail** | If warning appears, back out and try again after months of heavier spending |

---

## Key Warnings

- **Family language is permanent** — getting Platinum first kills Green and Gold welcome bonuses forever
- **Business cards are separate** — family rule doesn't cross over to business versions
- **NLL (No Lifetime Language) offers** are the only exception to once-per-lifetime — hunt for these targeted links
- **Co-branded cards** (Hilton, Delta, Marriott) — only worth it if you genuinely use that brand regularly

---

## Key Takeaway

> The American Express card ladder is a sequencing game. Get Green before Gold before Platinum — never skip ahead. Start with a no-fee entry card for 12+ months, then climb one rung at a time. The Gold Card alone nets you +$199/year in credits after the annual fee. The Platinum provides ~$1,790 in credits against an $895 fee. Violating the order permanently forfeits thousands in welcome bonuses.
