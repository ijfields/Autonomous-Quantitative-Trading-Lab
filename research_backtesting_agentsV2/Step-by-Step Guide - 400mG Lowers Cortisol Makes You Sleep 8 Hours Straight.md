## What This Guide Covers

How to use magnesium supplementation and lifestyle changes to stop the 2–3 AM cortisol spike that causes nighttime waking, visceral fat storage, and hormonal disruption.

---

## Prerequisites

- Magnesium supplement (glycinate, threonate, or taurate form — NOT oxide or citrate)
- 300–400 mg elemental dosage per serving
- Access to morning sunlight

---

## Step 1: Understand the Root Cause

Your 2–3 AM wake-up is NOT a sleep problem — it's a hormonal cascade:

1. Blood sugar drops during sleep
2. Brain perceives this as a threat (HPA axis activation)
3. Adrenals fire cortisol to raise blood sugar via gluconeogenesis
4. Cortisol suppresses melatonin → you wake up fully alert
5. Growth hormone, testosterone, and fat burning all shut down

**Why common fixes fail:**
- Melatonin can't override cortisol
- Sleeping pills sedate but don't fix the hormone trigger
- Breathing exercises don't correct mineral deficiency

## Step 3: Dose and Timing

- **Dose:** 300–400 mg elemental magnesium
- **Timing:** 60–90 minutes before bed
- **Why this window:** Magnesium needs time to activate GABA receptors and suppress adrenal output before the cortisol spike window (2–3 AM)
- **Consistency:** Take every night — effects compound over 1–4 weeks

---

## Step 4: Anchor Your Circadian Rhythm

**Morning sunlight exposure** within 10–20 minutes of waking:

1. Go outside (even on overcast days)
2. No sunglasses — light needs to enter through the eyes
3. This anchors cortisol to the morning (where it belongs)
4. High cortisol AM + low cortisol PM = healthy rhythm

> "Magnesium regulates the output. Sunlight sets the timing."

---

## Step 5: Stabilize Blood Sugar at Dinner

The blood sugar crash at 2–3 AM is what triggers the cortisol alarm. Prevent it:

1. Eat dinner **3+ hours before bed**
2. Include **30–40g protein** in the last meal
3. Minimize carbohydrates at dinner
4. If you eat carb-heavy, take a **10–15 minute walk** after the meal
5. Avoid late-night snacking entirely

---

## Step 6: Reduce Inflammatory Load

Inflammation increases cortisol demand, burning through magnesium faster:

- Eliminate vegetable/seed oils
- Avoid packaged and boxed foods
- Eat single-ingredient whole foods
- If you can't pronounce the ingredients, don't eat it
