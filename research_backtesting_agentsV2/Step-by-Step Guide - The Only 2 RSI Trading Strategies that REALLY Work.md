## Prerequisites
- **Tools:** Charting platform with RSI indicator (TradingView, etc.)
- **Knowledge:** RSI basics, support/resistance, candlestick patterns, Fibonacci retracements
- **Settings:** RSI set to **21 periods** (not the default 14)

---

## Strategy 1: RSI Divergence Reversal

### Step 1: Set RSI to 21 Periods
Change the default RSI from 14 to 21. This produces fewer false signals based on the speaker's experience over years in the markets.

### Step 2: Identify Divergence
- **Bearish divergence:** Price making higher highs while RSI makes lower highs
- **Bullish divergence:** Price making lower lows while RSI makes higher lows
- This is a **signal**, not yet a trigger to trade

### Step 3: Wait for Confirmation
Do NOT trade on divergence alone. Wait for:
1. Price breaks below support (bearish) or above resistance (bullish)
2. RSI breaks below the 50% zone (bearish) or above 50% (bullish)
- Both conditions should align before entering

### Step 4: Enter the Trade
- Enter at the confirmed breakout
- Example: Bearish divergence + price breaks below support + RSI below 50 → short entry

### Step 5: Set Target and Stop-Loss
- **Target:** Previous order block / price zone (the origin of the prior significant move)
- **Stop-loss:** Slightly above the broken support zone (bearish) or below broken resistance (bullish)
- Risk/reward should be excellent due to the distance to the order block target

## Strategy 2: RSI 50% Bounce Trend Following

### Step 1: Identify Established Trend
Look for a clear uptrend (or downtrend) with a large momentum candle confirming direction.

### Step 2: Wait for Pullback to Support
- Former resistance becomes support after a breakout
- Wait for price to pull back and retest this level

### Step 3: Confirm with Multiple Signals
Look for convergence of:
1. **Bullish confirmation candle** at the support level
2. **RSI bounces off the 50% zone** (does not break below it)
3. **Optional:** Fibonacci 61.8% retracement aligns with the support zone

### Step 4: Enter at Confirmation Candle Close
- Enter at the close of the 15-minute confirmation candle on the support level
- Do NOT enter before the candle closes

### Step 5: Set Stop-Loss and Target
- **Stop-loss:** Just below the confirmation candle / price zone
- **Target:** Minimum 2:1 risk/reward (risk $50 → target $100+)
- **Continuation:** When price breaks prior high, add to position

## Common Pitfalls
- **Buying oversold RSI blindly:** RSI in the 30% zone does NOT mean the market will bounce — it can keep dropping
- **Using RSI(14):** The standard 14-period gives too many false signals; switch to 21
- **Trading divergence without confirmation:** Divergence warns, but requires a price action trigger
- **Ignoring the 50% zone:** This is the key RSI level dividing bullish from bearish conditions
- **Setting random targets:** Always use previous price zones / order blocks as targets

## Summary
Two RSI strategies using RSI(21) instead of the standard 14-period setting. Strategy 1 trades divergence reversals with support/resistance breakout confirmation and targets at order blocks. Strategy 2 follows existing trends by entering on pullbacks to support when RSI bounces off 50% with optional Fibonacci confluence. Both require price action confirmation — never trade RSI signals alone.
