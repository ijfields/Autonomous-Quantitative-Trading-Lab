## Step 3: Apply Anti-Binary Thinking

**Principle:** Almost nothing in reality is truly binary (0 or 1). AI trained on human data inherits our binary thinking habits.

**How to implement:**
1. When AI presents option A or option B, ask: "What are options C and D?"
2. In Resonant OS, the "Liberation from Binary Thinking" protocol runs automatically
3. AI will propose alternatives beyond the obvious binary choices
4. Applies to: investment decisions, code architecture, strategy selection, problem diagnosis

**Example:** Instead of "Should I go long or short?" → AI explores: hedge, wait, scale in, use options, take no position, etc.

## Step 4: Use Dissonance as a Compass

**Principle:** When something feels "off" about AI output, don't ignore it — investigate it.

**How to implement:**
1. When AI produces output that feels plausible but uncomfortable, say: "I have some dissonance with this part"
2. AI will investigate WHY the dissonance exists instead of people-pleasing ("you're totally right")
3. This turns friction into productive exploration
4. Applies to: code reviews, strategy evaluation, research analysis

---

## Step 5: Adopt the Gardener Mindset

**Principle:** A good gardener tends the soil, not the plants. Good output emerges from good preparation.

**How to implement:**
1. When starting a new project, DON'T ask AI for the deliverable immediately
2. Instead, ask AI to:
   - Explore the problem space
   - Identify potentialities and weak points
   - Collect relevant data and context
   - Surface your assumptions and fears
3. Let the solution EMERGE from thorough understanding
4. Only then produce the deliverable (business plan, strategy, code)

**Anti-pattern:** "Build me a trading bot" → AI immediately produces code
**Gardener approach:** "Let's explore what kind of trading bot would work for my situation" → discuss, research, understand → bot design emerges naturally

## Step 6: Inject Critical Documents (Single Source of Truth)

**Principle:** Don't rely on RAG/retrieval for important context. Put key documents directly in AI's view.

**How to implement:**
1. Identify your **Single Sources of Truth** (SSoT): business plan, strategy rules, coding standards, etc.
2. Use Resonant OS's injection feature to place these documents directly into AI's active context
3. AI processes them as if they were always known — no retrieval needed
4. Injected documents take priority over vague memories or retrieved fragments

**Why this matters:** Traditional RAG systems may retrieve wrong context or miss key details. Injection ensures AI can "see" the document directly, reducing hallucination risk significantly.

## Step 7: Set Up Logician Gates

**Principle:** AI claims to have completed tasks ~42% of the time when it actually hasn't. Verify, don't trust.

**How to implement:**
1. Resonant OS includes a Logician — a software-based (non-LLM) verification system
2. The Logician creates gates that check:
   - "I asked you to create a file — show me the file"
   - "I asked you to use this tool — show evidence"
   - "I asked you to save this — verify it was saved"
3. Gates force AI to prove completion before moving to the next step
4. **Self-learning:** When the same mistake happens multiple times, the Logician automatically creates a new gate to prevent recurrence

## Step 8: Add Intentional Friction to Creative Work

**Principle:** AI operates in a frictionless, instant-speed world. Humans need friction for creativity.

**How to implement:**
1. Identify which parts of your workflow require creativity vs. production
2. For creative phases: use AI for brainstorming, analyzing, exploring — NOT producing at speed
3. Sleep on important decisions — don't let AI's speed push you into premature commitments
4. For production phases: let AI work at full speed

**Key insight:** Friction is not inefficiency — it's where creativity lives.
