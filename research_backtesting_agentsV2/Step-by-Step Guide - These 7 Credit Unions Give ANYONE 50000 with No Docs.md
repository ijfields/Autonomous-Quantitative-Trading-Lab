## What This Guide Covers

How to obtain no-doc business lines of credit (BLOCs) from seven specific credit unions that don't require tax returns, bank statements, or P&Ls — using a bureau-aware sequencing strategy.

---

## Prerequisites

- Good personal credit scores (all 3 bureaus)
- A registered business entity (LLC, Corp, etc.)
- Understanding of your current hard inquiry count per bureau

---

## Step 1: Assess Your Credit Profile

Before applying:
1. Pull all three credit reports (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion)
2. Note your score on each bureau
3. Count existing hard inquiries on each bureau
4. Identify which bureau has the fewest recent inquiries — apply there first

## Step 3: Plan Your Bureau Sequence

**Goal:** 1–2 hard pulls per bureau maximum.

**Example stacking sequence:**

1. **Experian pull** → Apply at SE Federal or FirstTech (within 30-day window, get both card + BLOC)
2. **Equifax pull** → Apply at Langley FCU (within 60-day window, get both card + BLOC)
3. **TransUnion pull** → Apply at Alliant (within 60-day window, get both card + BLOC)

**Theoretical max from 3 hard pulls:** $100K + $75K + $100K = **$275,000**

---

## Step 4: Apply Within Each Window

Each credit union allows ONE hard pull that's valid for a set period:

1. Apply for the **business credit card** first
2. Within the validity window (30 or 60 days), apply for the **business line of credit**
3. Both products use the same single hard pull
4. Do NOT apply for card and BLOC at separate institutions on the same bureau

---

## Step 5: Key Rules

- **No docs required:** These credit unions base decisions on personal credit scores, not financials
- **Max exposure is per institution:** The combined cap applies to card + BLOC together
- **Don't exceed the window:** If you miss the 30 or 60-day window, you'll need a new hard pull
- **Bureau accuracy:** Justice FCU has mixed reports (some say TransUnion, some Equifax) — confirm with a banker before applying
