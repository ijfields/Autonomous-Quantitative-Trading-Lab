# Step-by-Step Guide: Supporting the TargetFast Boycott & Redirecting Spending to Black-Owned Businesses

**Source:** Roland S. Martin (YouTube)
**Video ID:** yJH1pKfD76c
**Upload Date:** 2025-04-23

---

## What This Guide Covers

Actionable steps from the TargetFast Town Hall for participating in the Target boycott and redirecting consumer spending toward Black-owned businesses, lenders, and media.

---

## Step 1: Join the Boycott

1. Register at **targetfast.org** for updates and coordination
2. Commit to a sustained boycott — not a temporary pause
3. Understand the "why": Target voluntarily pledged $2B to Black businesses, $100M in scholarships, 20% workforce increase, and $10M to social justice — then walked away from all of it

---

## Step 2: Find Black-Owned Alternatives

1. Go to **byblack.us** (BYBLACKUS) — directory of 150,000+ verified Black-owned businesses
2. Search by category for alternatives to products you currently buy at Target
3. Check out **spadeslife.com** — new digital marketplace for Black consumers (shaped like Amazon/Etsy)
4. Look for Black-owned options in every spending category: groceries, household, personal care, clothing

---

## Step 3: Pay Full Price

1. Stop asking for "the brother hookup or the homegirl love" — pay what's on the tag
2. Black businesses need full-price revenue to grow, hire, and reinvest
3. Apply the same standard you apply to corporations: if you pay full price at Target, pay full price at a Black-owned business

---

## Step 4: Support Black Financial Infrastructure

1. Use **Lindustry** for lending — described as the largest Black lender in America with competitive rates
2. Visit **usblackchambers.org** for:
   - Manufacturing grants (available now)
   - Business support resources
   - Technical assistance
3. If you own a business, list it on BYBLACKUS

---

## Step 5: Support Black Media

1. Subscribe to Black newspapers (local and national)
2. Follow and share content on **Fanbase** (Black-owned social media platform by Isaac Hayes III)
3. Watch/stream via Black-owned channels like Roland S. Martin's platform
4. Sustained consciousness requires sustained information flow

---

## Step 6: Support Service Businesses, Not Just Retail

1. Black-owned service businesses (printing, consulting, accounting, legal, tech) need support too
2. Keep the dollar circulating within the ecosystem — hire Black contractors, professionals, and freelancers
3. Consolidate support around existing organizations rather than creating new ones:
   - Buy From a Black Woman
   - US Black Chambers
   - Buy Black initiatives already in your city

---

## Step 7: Educate and Include

1. Build home libraries with banned books — take responsibility for educating children about their history
2. Support Black educational initiatives and scholarships directly
3. Include all Black people in the movement — LGBTQ+ members, young people, elders
4. As Fannie Lou Hamer said: "None of us are free unless all of us are free"

---

## Step 8: Engage Young People

1. Share the movement on social media — study how viral content works
2. Build digital platforms and content that amplifies Black economic power
3. Young people (under 35) are already searching for Black businesses daily — meet them where they are

---

## Step 9: If You're a Black Business Owner

1. List your business on **byblack.us**
2. Apply for manufacturing grants at **usblackchambers.org**
3. Explore lending through **Lindustry** for expansion capital
4. Address supply chain vulnerabilities — look for domestic manufacturing sources for components
5. Connect with local Black chambers of commerce for networking and technical assistance

---

## Key Takeaway

> "Opening a store in the Black community is not an investment in Black communities — it's just another opportunity to make money." The boycott is not the end goal. It's a tool to demonstrate collective power while building permanent infrastructure for Black economic self-determination. Target is being made an example because they made the promises voluntarily — and broke them.
