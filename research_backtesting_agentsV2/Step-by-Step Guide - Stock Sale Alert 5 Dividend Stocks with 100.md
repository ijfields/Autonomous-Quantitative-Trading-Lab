# Step-by-Step Guide: Building a Dividend Portfolio with $100 Increments

**Source:** Mommy Trader (YouTube)
**Video ID:** Ba3XbJzSgcY
**Upload Date:** 2025-03-14

---

## What This Guide Covers

How to build a dividend portfolio using fractional shares and consistent small investments, based on Chalice's approach that grew a ~$40K-$45K portfolio from $20-at-a-time purchases.

---

## Step 1: Open a Brokerage Account with Fractional Shares

1. Choose a platform that supports fractional share purchases (Robinhood, Fidelity, Schwab, etc.)
2. Enable fractional share buying so you can invest any dollar amount
3. No minimum investment required — even $1 counts

---

## Step 2: Select Dividend-Focused Securities

Build a diversified portfolio across categories:

| Category | Example Ticker | Purpose |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| Monthly dividend ETF | SPYI | Regular income, S&P 500 exposure |
| Dividend growth ETF | DJRO / SCHD | Growth + dividends, tech exposure |
| Monthly dividend stock | MAIN (Main Street Capital) | Individual stock with monthly + special quarterly dividends |
| Blue-chip growth | V (Visa) | Capital appreciation + small dividend |
| International diversification | VXUS | Global exposure outside US |

---

## Step 3: Set Share-Count Goals

1. For each position, set a target number of shares (e.g., 200 shares of SPYI)
2. Track current shares vs goal in a spreadsheet or planner
3. Having a concrete target keeps you motivated during market pullbacks
4. Adjust goals upward as positions grow

---

## Step 4: Invest Consistently

1. Allocate a fixed amount per investment session (video uses $100 split 5 ways = $20 each)
2. Buy regardless of market conditions — this is dollar-cost averaging
3. Buy more aggressively during pullbacks: "When markets are red, that's a sale on good assets"
4. Use tax refunds, side hustle income, and bonuses to accelerate

---

## Step 5: Track Everything

1. Maintain a portfolio tracker (spreadsheet, app, or physical planner)
2. Record: shares owned, cost basis, current value, unrealized gain/loss, dividends received
3. Review monthly to see progress toward share-count goals
4. Tracking is the "secret sauce" for maintaining motivation during downturns

---

## Step 6: Reinvest Dividends

1. Enable DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plan) if your broker supports it
2. Alternatively, accumulate dividends and manually reinvest during pullbacks
3. Compounding dividends accelerates portfolio growth over years

---

## Step 7: Stay the Course

1. Every market pullback in history has recovered to new all-time highs
2. Resist the urge to sell during fear and panic
3. Consistency over years beats timing the market
4. Action matters more than affirmations — the $20 investments add up

---

## Key Takeaway

> You can build a substantial dividend portfolio from $20-at-a-time purchases using fractional shares. Chalice grew ~$40K-$45K across 5 positions by investing consistently over several years. The system is simple: select quality dividend securities, set share-count goals, invest a fixed amount regularly regardless of market conditions, track everything, and reinvest dividends.
