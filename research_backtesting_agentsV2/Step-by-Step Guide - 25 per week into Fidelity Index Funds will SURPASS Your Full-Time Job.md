## Step 1: Understand Why Weekly Investing Works

- **Inflation erodes cash:** Essentials rise 4-5%/yr; wages only 3.5-4%
- **Stock market averages ~10-12%/yr** over 100+ years — 2-3x faster than wage growth
- **Dollar-cost averaging (DCA):** When market drops, your $25 buys more shares; when it rises, you benefit from growth
- **Compounding is exponential:** Early years look flat, but the curve steepens dramatically in years 20-30

## Step 2: Open a Fidelity Account

1. Go to **Fidelity.com** and create a brokerage account
2. Choose an **Individual Brokerage Account** (or Roth IRA for tax-free growth)
3. Link your bank account for weekly transfers
4. Enable **fractional shares** (allows investing exact dollar amounts)

## Step 4: Enable Automatic Weekly Investment

1. In Fidelity, go to **Accounts & Trade → Automatic Investments**
2. Set up a **recurring weekly purchase** for each fund
3. Amount: **$5 per fund** (or adjust to your budget)
4. Schedule: Pick the same day each week (e.g., every Monday)
5. Funding: Link to your bank account for automatic transfers

---

## Step 5: Enable Dividend Reinvestment (DRIP)

1. In Fidelity, go to **Account Features → Dividends and Capital Gains**
2. Set each fund to **"Reinvest in Security"**
3. This ensures all dividends automatically buy more shares
4. The reinvestment cycle: dividends → more shares → more dividends → compounding accelerates

---

## Step 6: Adopt the Right Success Metric

**Wrong question:** "How much did my portfolio grow this week?"
**Right question:** "Did I invest this week?"

- **Years 1-3:** Balance barely moves — this is normal
- **Years 5-10:** Growth becomes visible
- **Years 20-30:** Exponential curve — growth outpaces contributions
- **Don't check daily.** Set it, automate it, and review quarterly at most

## Step 8: Stay the Course

- **Market crashes are buying opportunities** — your $25 buys more shares at lower prices
- **Don't sell during downturns** — every historical crash has recovered and gone higher
- **Avoid chasing hot stocks** — diversified index funds outperform most stock pickers
- **Re-evaluate annually** — check fund performance and rebalance if one fund drifts significantly from 20%
