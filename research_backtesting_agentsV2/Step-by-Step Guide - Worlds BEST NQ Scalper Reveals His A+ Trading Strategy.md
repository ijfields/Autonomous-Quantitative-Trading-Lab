## Step 1: Daily Chart Analysis (Morning Prep)

**Goal:** Identify the daily dealing range and where 50% sits.

1. Open the NQ daily chart
2. Mark the current swing high and swing low that form the active dealing range
3. Draw the 50% (equilibrium) level between them
4. Determine where you expect the daily candle to form its wick:
   - **Bearish bias:** Expect wick above previous day high, body closes lower → short opportunity
   - **Bullish bias:** Expect wick below previous day low, body closes higher → long opportunity
5. Note: if price hasn't reached 50% of a higher timeframe range, anticipate it will

**Key concept:** You want to trade the wick of the daily candle, not the body. The wick is the manipulation zone.

---

## Step 2: H4 Chart Analysis (Fractal Layer 2)

**Goal:** Identify the 4-hour P3 cycle within the daily context.

1. Drop to the H4 chart
2. Mark the previous 4-hour candle's high and low
3. Identify where 50% of the 4-hour range sits
4. Look for price building up engineered liquidity around the 4-hour EQ (equilibrium):
   - Multiple highs/lows clustering near the same level = liquidity buildup
   - Failed attempts to distribute into EQ = each failure creates a new range to trade
5. The 4-hour wick zone is where you want to be positioned for entry

**Key concept:** The H4 P3 should nest inside the Daily P3. If the daily wick forms above a previous high, the H4 should be forming its own wick in the same area.

## Step 3: H1 Chart Analysis (Fractal Layer 3 -- Entry Timeframe)

**Goal:** Pinpoint the hourly P3 cycle for precise entry.

1. Drop to the H1 chart
2. Mark the previous hourly candle's high (especially the 9:00 AM hour high)
3. Identify the 50% level of the hourly range
4. Wait for the hourly P3 to align with both the daily and H4:
   - **Accumulation:** Price builds a range during pre-market / early session
   - **Manipulation:** Price sweeps above the 9:00 AM hour high at ~10:00 AM
   - **Distribution:** Price reverses and trades back through the range

**Key concept:** When all three timeframes (Daily, H4, H1) show their P3 wick zones overlapping, that convergence zone is where you want to enter.

## Step 4: Wait for 10:00 AM Manipulation

**Goal:** Let the market show its hand before entering.

1. Between 9:15-10:00 AM EST, observe the market but do NOT trade
2. The 9:30 AM open creates a liquidity injection -- mark where the initial move goes
3. At approximately 10:00 AM, look for price to:
   - Sweep above the previous 9:00 AM hour high (for shorts)
   - Sweep below the previous 9:00 AM hour low (for longs)
4. This sweep is the "manipulation" phase of the hourly P3
5. Do NOT enter yet -- manipulation alone is not enough

**Key concept:** "Everybody will always tell me 10 AM reversal doesn't work. Fine, then don't trade it." The 10 AM manipulation is a statistical tendency, not a certainty. Confirmation is still needed.

## Step 5: Confirm with SMT Divergence (NQ vs ES)

**Goal:** Verify the manipulation is real using inter-market divergence.

1. Compare NQ and ES at the manipulation point:
   - **Bearish setup:** ES sweeps the high but NQ fails to sweep it → NQ shows relative weakness
   - **Bullish setup:** ES sweeps the low but NQ fails to sweep it → NQ shows relative strength
2. This divergence confirms the manipulation is exhausted
3. "Dual SMT" (divergence at both highs AND lows) = even stronger signal (range tightening)
4. Your invalidation: the divergence high (for shorts) should NOT be broken going forward

**Key concept:** The divergence between NQ and ES is the confirmation that liquidity has been taken and the real move is about to begin. Without this, do NOT enter.

## Step 6: Enter at the Inversion Level

**Goal:** Execute the trade with defined risk.

1. Identify an inversion level in the manipulation zone:
   - An inversion = a previous fair value gap (FVG) or imbalance that acted as support, now being used as resistance (or vice versa)
2. Choose your entry method:
   - **Sell stop:** Place below the inversion level -- fills when price breaks through
   - **Limit order:** Wait for price to retap the inversion level from below after initial breakdown
3. Place stop loss at the divergence point (above the manipulation high for shorts)
4. Set take profit at 50% of the dealing range (the equilibrium level of the next range down)

**Entry summary for shorts:**
- Entry: at or below the inversion level
- Stop: above the SMT divergence high
- Target: 50% of the range (EQ)

## Step 7: Aggressive Break-Even Management

**Goal:** Protect capital immediately once the trade moves in your favor.

1. Once the hourly candle flips bearish (for shorts) or bullish (for longs) → **move stop to break even**
2. When the 15-minute low is taken (for shorts) → also a break-even trigger
3. "Right or right out" -- if the trade isn't working immediately, exit
4. Do NOT hold hoping for a reversal if the candle flips back against you

**Break-even rules:**
- Hourly candle flips in your direction → break even
- 15-minute structural level broken in your direction → break even
- If stopped at break even: no loss, no gain -- this is a GOOD outcome
- Re-entry is allowed if the model hasn't been invalidated (bias unchanged)

## Step 8: Take Profit or Trail

**Goal:** Exit with the base hit or trail for a larger move.

**Default (base hit):**
- Close the trade at 50% of the range
- Walk away, done for the day
- This is the bread and butter -- 3-4 winning days out of 5

**Runner (discretionary, intuition-based):**
- If price blasts through 50% with high volume / momentum
- Trail stop loss to the 50% level (your original target)
- Continue trailing aggressively on each new structural break
- If stopped out on the trail, you've already locked in the base hit profit
- Only do this after you've built confidence through months of reps

## Step 9: Re-Entry Rules (If Stopped Out)

**Goal:** Know when to take a second shot and when to walk away.

1. If stopped at break even and the model is still valid (bias unchanged, no new highs breaking divergence):
   - Wait for a new SMT divergence signal
   - Re-enter with the same setup
   - Same stop and target rules apply
2. If stopped at a loss and the manipulation high was broken:
   - The model is invalidated for this session
   - Do NOT re-enter in the same direction
   - Wait for the next trading day or look for the opposite direction in the PM session (~2:00 PM EST)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Trading without all three timeframes aligned** -- skipping the daily or H4 means trading without context
2. **Entering before SMT divergence confirms** -- manipulation alone is not enough
3. **Not managing break-evens aggressively** -- this is what makes the model profitable
4. **Trying to catch the extreme (low of day)** instead of taking the base hit -- leads to round-trips
5. **Trading the open (9:30 AM)** without confirmation -- too volatile, no P3 structure yet
6. **Holding losers** hoping they'll reverse -- "right or right out"
7. **Evaluating the model on a single week** -- 3-6 months minimum for statistical validity
8. **Ignoring the fractal nature** -- the model works on ANY timeframe/asset, not just NQ intraday
