## KEY TOPICS

### The Business Model

- **2008 STOCK Act loophole:** Politicians must file every stock trade publicly
- Politicians allegedly continued insider trading even after mandated disclosure
- Autopilot connects to users' brokerages (Robinhood, Public, etc.) and automatically buys/sells based on politician trades
- Originally 90% revenue from Pelosi tracker; now diversified to 30–40%
- **$18.5M ARR**, best month: $2.8M (January)
- **Revenue growth:** ~150% YoY in 2023–2024, then 300–400% recently

### Onboarding: The Magic Moment Framework

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Magic moment = first trade** | Stocks are bought in user's account BEFORE showing the paywall |
| **Time constraint** | Onboarding must complete in "bathroom time" (~90 seconds) |
| **Paywall after value** | If paywall comes before magic moment, conversion drops 70% |
| **Forced magic moment** | "We'd rather people not use the app than not experience the magic moment" |
| **Emotional test** | If test users don't say "Wow, this is incredible" unprompted, it's not strong enough |

### Mental Calories Framework

- Every onboarding screen has a "cognitive load" budget
- High-anxiety screens (connecting brokerage) are allowed more mental calories
- Low-anxiety screens must be stripped to bare minimum
- **iOS app detection:** App checks which brokerages the user has installed (up to 50 apps visible), only shows those — reduces choice overload
- **Binary decisions:** Make every question yes/no when possible
- **Limit options:** Show 4 portfolios (not hundreds) — showing all = 70% less likely to decide

### The $100K Income Gate

- Screen asks: "Will you make more than $100,000 this year? Yes or No?"
- If "No" → blocked from app, but can go back and change answer
- **Result:** People who initially said "No" then said "Yes" paid at a **70% higher rate**
- **Psychology:** App feels like an exclusive club; people who "shouldn't be there" complain less and value it more

### Hiring Philosophy

- **Curiosity and obsession** are the primary hiring signals
- Interview question: "What belief do you hold that people around you disagree with?"
- Curious people have multiple, well-thought-out answers and keep talking
- **Historical obsession:** Look for anything done at an elite level (D1 college sports, competitive guitar, etc.)
- D1 athletes specifically valued: can be pushed hard without taking it personally + proven obsession
- **Work expectations:** 50–60 hours/week, but constant thinking/curiosity outside work hours
- Full-time recruiter hired at 15 employees (ex-TikTok) — improved from 25% to 50% success rate

### Firing and Severance

- For every 4 hires, 3 were fired initially (now 2:1 ratio)
- **3-month severance** for everyone, even 2-week employees
- Inspired by Netflix co-founder: generous severance makes it easier to maintain high standards
- "Call to persuade them NOT to join" — transparent about high turnover culture
- People who get excited by the challenge tend to work out; hesitant ones generally don't

### 6x Super-User Monetization (StubHub Founder Advice)

- Jeff (StubHub founder, their VC) pointed out: "Users can only decay — you can't make more from existing customers"
- **StubHub rule:** 80% of revenue from 20% of customers (super users)
- Users should be able to pay up to **6x their initial subscription** or more
- Autopilot went from single $100/year plan to marketplace model (up to $5,000/year possible)
- **12-month revenue retention jumped from 60–70% to 110%** (net positive from upsells)

## ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

1. **Engineer the "magic moment" before the paywall** — let users experience core value first, then charge
2. **Onboarding should complete in ~90 seconds** — "if they can't finish while going to the bathroom, it's too long"
3. **Reduce mental calories per screen** — detect context (installed apps), limit choices to 4, make questions binary
4. **Exclusivity gates increase conversion** — the $100K income screen raised paid conversion 70% by making the app feel like a club
5. **Hire for curiosity and obsession, not resume** — look for elite performance in ANY domain
6. **Offer 3-month severance to maintain high hiring bar** — makes fast firing sustainable and culturally acceptable
7. **Enable 6x super-user spend** — revenue retention becomes net-positive when top users can pay multiples of base subscription
8. **Revenue retention > subscription retention** — 110% 12-month revenue retention is the real metric
