## KEY TOPICS

### The 2–3 AM Cortisol Spike Mechanism

- **Two opposing hormones:** Cortisol (morning) and melatonin (night) — they cannot coexist
- Blood sugar drops during sleep → body senses it as dangerous → adrenals fire cortisol as emergency alarm
- Cortisol raises blood sugar by pulling glucose from the liver (gluconeogenesis)
- Result: wide awake at 2–3 AM, not groggy — cortisol is activating
- Chinese medicine calls this "liver time" — liver is most active for fat burning, bile recycling, detox

### Consequences of Nighttime Cortisol

| Effect | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Blocks growth hormone | Cortisol suppresses HGH release during deep sleep |
| Raises insulin | Promotes insulin resistance |
| Increases visceral fat | 2–3x more cortisol receptor sites in belly fat vs other areas |
| Suppresses testosterone | Bad for both men and women |
| Elevates inflammatory markers | HS-CRP, IL-6, cytokines |

### Why Common Sleep Advice Fails

- **Melatonin supplements:** Don't override cortisol — if cortisol spikes, melatonin loses
- **Breathing exercises:** Don't correct a mineral deficiency
- **Sleeping pills:** Sedate the brain but don't regulate hormones or address root cause

### Magnesium: The Brake Pedal

- 70–80% of people are deficient (per multiple human studies)
- Responsible for 400+ enzymatic activities in the body
- Functions: regulates adrenal output, activates GABA (calming neurotransmitter), converts serotonin → melatonin, activates parasympathetic nervous system

### The Vicious Cycle

Stress → magnesium depletion → higher cortisol → worse sleep → more magnesium loss → repeat

**Magnesium burners:** Stress, cheap coffee, alcohol, poor sleep, intense workouts (especially near bedtime)

## THE PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Magnesium Supplementation
- **Dose:** 300–400 mg elemental magnesium
- **Timing:** 60–90 minutes before bed
- **Forms:** Glycinate, threonate, or taurate
- **Avoid:** Magnesium oxide (poorly absorbed), magnesium citrate (bowel release — may wake you to pee)
- **Why timing matters:** Too late = miss the cortisol suppression window

### Step 2: Morning Sunlight
- Within 10–20 minutes of waking
- Even if overcast — no sunglasses
- Anchors cortisol rhythm: high in morning (within range), low at night
- "Magnesium regulates output. Sunlight sets the timing."

### Step 3: Blood Sugar Stabilization at Dinner
- Last meal at least 3 hours before bed
- Include 30–40g protein
- Avoid excessive carbohydrates at night
- If carb-heavy dinner, take a 10–15 minute walk after
- Prevents blood sugar spike → crash → adrenal alarm

### Step 4: Reduce Inflammatory Foods
- Avoid ingredients you can't pronounce
- Avoid vegetable/seed oils, packaged/boxed foods
- Eat single-ingredient whole foods
- Inflammation increases cortisol demand
