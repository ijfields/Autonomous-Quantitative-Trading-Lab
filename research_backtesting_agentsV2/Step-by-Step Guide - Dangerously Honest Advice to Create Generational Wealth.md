## What This Guide Covers

The four-step sequence actual wealthy families follow — and the two popular tax fraud schemes to avoid.

---

## Prerequisites

- A business or income stream (or the intention to build one)
- Basic understanding of income tax vs. estate tax distinction

---

## Step 1: Identify and Avoid Tax Fraud Schemes

### Scheme A: Spendthrift Trust (Section 643 Misread)
- **Red flag:** Anyone selling a "non-grantor irrevocable complex discretionary trust" that claims to eliminate income tax
- **Reality:** Section 643 only determines allocation between trust and beneficiaries — the income is STILL taxed (the trust pays if not distributed)
- **IRS guidance:** Formally clarified in 2023

### Scheme B: 508(c)(1)(A) Church/Ministry Trust
- **Red flag:** Anyone selling a "faith-based foundation" or "legacy church" to route business income
- **Reality:** Only applies to actual churches meeting strict IRS requirements — independent governance, public religious purpose, no private inurement

---

## Step 2: Build Real Economic Value

1. Focus all energy on growing a business or income stream
2. Choose an area where you have an actual competitive advantage
3. Do NOT spend time on entity structures, trusts, or tax planning at this stage
4. Reinvest profits back into the business until it no longer needs every dollar

---

## Step 3: Shift Excess Capital Into Tax-Advantaged Assets

1. Once income is consistent and the business is self-sustaining, deploy excess capital
2. **Primary vehicle: Real estate** — offers depreciation, long-term appreciation, and tax-deferred exchanges
3. Use **1031 exchanges** to move between properties without triggering taxable events
4. Only invest in assets you understand

---

## Step 4: Plan at the Right Time

1. Engage estate planning (trusts, FLPs, entity optimization) ONLY when:
   - Income is consistent
   - Assets are material
   - You're approaching the **~$15M per person** estate tax exemption threshold
2. Tools at this stage: irrevocable trusts, family limited partnerships, entity optimization
3. These protect and transfer wealth — they do not create it

---

## Key Warnings

- **Premature complexity kills:** Locking assets into irrevocable structures too early creates income tax inefficiencies and forfeits step-up in basis
- **The IRS catches up:** Tax schemes take years to unwind but the outcome is always reversed positions, penalties, interest, and prosecuted promoters
- **The wrapper is not the wealth:** Trusts, entities, and structures are tools for post-wealth management, not pre-wealth creation

---

## Key Takeaway

> Position sizing — not entries and exits — determines profitability. Similarly, value creation — not tax structures — determines wealth. Build value first, reinvest wisely second, shift to tax-advantaged assets third, and plan estates last. Gimmicks never.
