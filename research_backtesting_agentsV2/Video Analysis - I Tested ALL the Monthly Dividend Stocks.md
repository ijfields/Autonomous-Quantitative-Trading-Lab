## Overview
A data-driven analysis of all 44 monthly dividend-paying stocks, comparing both dividend income and total return over 1-year (2023) and 5-year (2019–2023) periods. Uses a $10,000 lump-sum investment with full dividend reinvestment and no additional contributions as the test basis.

---

## Key Concepts

### High Yield ≠ High Return
The central finding is that dividend yield is a poor predictor of total return. Stocks yielding 15-21% often had the worst portfolio performance, while some moderate-yield stocks delivered strong total returns.

### Income vs. Total Return
The video distinguishes between:
- **Dividend income:** Cash generated from dividends alone
- **Portfolio balance (total return):** Ending value including price appreciation + reinvested dividends
- A stock can rank well for income but poorly for total return if its share price declines significantly

### Consistent vs. Inconsistent Performers
Cross-referencing 1-year and 5-year results reveals which stocks are reliable versus which had anomalous results in either direction.

## Backtestable Parameters
- **Universe:** All U.S.-listed stocks paying monthly dividends (44 stocks as of 2024)
- **Test setup:** $10,000 lump sum, full DRIP, no additional contributions
- **Metrics:** Total return (price + dividends), dividend income only, ending portfolio balance
- **Time horizons:** 1 year, 5 years
- **Potential filters to backtest:**
  - Exclude stocks where 5-year total return < initial investment
  - Exclude stocks where dividend yield > 15% (value trap screen)
  - Require positive total return in both 1-year AND 5-year windows

## Actionable Takeaways
1. **Don't chase yield** — the highest-yielding monthly dividend stocks (15-21%) were among the worst performers for total return
2. **Gladstone Investment (GAIN)** is the standout — strong performance across income and total return in both time periods
3. **Always check total return**, not just dividend income — a high-income stock losing 40% of its value is a net loser
4. **Use 5-year minimum** for evaluation — 1-year results can be misleading (PBT was #1 over 5 years but terrible in 2023 alone)
5. **Consider alternatives** — quarterly dividend stocks, ETFs, or covered call strategies may outperform the monthly dividend stock universe
