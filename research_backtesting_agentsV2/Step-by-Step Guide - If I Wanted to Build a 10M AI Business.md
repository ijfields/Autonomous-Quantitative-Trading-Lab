# Step-by-Step Guide: Building a $10M AI Business with Zero Employees

**Source:** Dan Martell (YouTube)
**Video ID:** w-XPlC3a2oI
**Upload Date:** 2025-03-14

---

## What This Guide Covers

Dan Martell's 6-step framework for building a one-person AI business from $0 to $10M+, with real examples including a company doing $83K MRR with just 1 founder and 2 part-time contractors.

---

## Step 1: Stop Throwing Bodies at Problems

1. Shift your mindset: revenue growth should NOT require proportional headcount growth
2. For every bottleneck, ask: "Can this be automated with AI?"
3. New model: identify bottleneck → automate → complexity shrinks as revenue grows
4. Your job is to design the system that does the work, not to do the work yourself

---

## Step 2: Find a Painful Problem Worth Solving

1. Fall in love with the customer's problem, NOT the technology
2. Look for "painkiller" problems (urgent, frequent, expensive) in growing markets:
   - AI/automation, healthcare, real estate, coaching, SaaS
3. Call 10 people in your target market for advice (not to sell)
4. Ask: "What's the most painful part of your business right now?"
5. Co-design the initial specs with these 10 conversations
6. The problem should be something people are already spending money to solve (badly)

---

## Step 3: Solve the Problem Manually First

1. Fix the problem by hand for 3-5 customers
2. Get paid to learn the steps — this validates demand AND teaches you the process
3. Document every step you take manually (this becomes your automation blueprint)
4. Draft a one-page offer with 5 elements:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Problem | What painful problem you solve |
| Promise | What outcome you guarantee |
| Timeline | How long it takes |
| Price | What it costs |
| Guarantee | Risk reversal for the customer |

5. Call back the 10 people from Step 2 and present the offer

---

## Step 4: Build a Clickable Prototype (Wizard of Oz)

1. Do NOT spend $50K building a real product yet
2. Build a fake-but-realistic prototype using:
   - **Figma** (design tool)
   - **UX Pilot.ai** (AI-powered mockup creation)
   - **Visily.ai** (describe screens in plain English, it builds them)
3. Test with 5 new customers (not the original 10)
4. Record their screen and reactions — watch what they click
5. "You'll learn more in five customer calls than in five weeks of coding"
6. Iterate the prototype based on feedback before building anything real

---

## Step 5: Build Your MVP (Minimum Viable Product)

1. Core features ONLY — resist all feature requests
2. Reference: Facebook started with one college, Amazon started with just books
3. Use an AI app builder like Manis AI to generate a full-stack app from a prompt
4. Prompt template:
   - Core promise (1 sentence)
   - 3 screens: login, data input, output/insights
   - Authentication included
   - Clean, minimal UI
   - No extra features
5. Iterate by talking to the AI builder like a person — describe what's wrong, what you want changed
6. Only add features that impact 80%+ of users

---

## Step 6: Scale with AI Agents, Not Headcount

| Revenue Stage | Model |
|---------------|-------|
| $0-$100K | You do everything; AI helps you move faster |
| $100K-$1M | Build systems AI can run: onboarding, support, operations |
| $1M-$10M | Stack AI agents and workflows; only loop yourself in for decisions |

1. At each stage, identify what YOU are still doing manually
2. Automate those tasks with AI agents and workflows
3. The metric to optimize: revenue per person (not team size)
4. Real example: Martell Ventures company → $83K MRR with 1 founder + 2 part-time contractors

---

## Key Takeaway

> "Complexity kills more businesses than competition." The framework: find a painful problem, solve it manually and get paid, build a clickable prototype before coding, build an MVP with core features only, and scale with AI agents instead of employees. Every step validates before investing — you never build something nobody wants.
