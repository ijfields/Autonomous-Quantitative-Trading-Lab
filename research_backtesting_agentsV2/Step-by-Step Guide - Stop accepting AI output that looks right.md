# Step-by-Step Guide: Building an AI Rejection Flywheel (Constraint Library)

**Source:** AI News & Strategy Daily | Nate B Jones (YouTube)
**Video ID:** -FhtPUkXKO4

---

## What This Guide Covers

How to build a systematic process for capturing, encoding, and scaling AI quality judgments — turning individual rejections of bad AI output into a compounding organizational asset.

---

## Step 1: Recognize — Develop Domain-Specific Detection

- AI output that "looks right" is the most dangerous kind
- Recognition depends on deep domain experience, not AI fluency
- A domain expert + AI can evaluate 10x the output — but only within their expertise boundary
- Outside that boundary, AI "multiplies confidence, not expertise"

---

## Step 2: Articulate — Turn Rejections into Constraints

| Bad Rejection | Good Constraint |
|--------------|-----------------|
| "This isn't right" | "This fails because you're treating all requirements identically — the PRD needs to be structured by priority tier" |
| "Redo this" | "The regulatory assumption in paragraph 3 doesn't account for the 2024 amendment to Section 12(b)" |
| "Not what I wanted" | "The tone is too formal for this audience — match the conversational style in our Q3 newsletter" |

---

## Step 3: Encode — Capture Constraints Durably

1. Set up an MCP server backed by a database (tool available on Nate's Substack)
2. Capture rejections as structured constraints without leaving your chat tool
3. Zero context switching — capture happens as a side effect of the rejection
4. Alternatively: start with a structured document or spreadsheet

### Constraint Structure
- **Domain:** (e.g., "legal review", "marketing copy", "financial modeling")
- **Rule:** The specific constraint
- **Reason:** Why this matters
- **Source:** Who contributed this (expert attribution)
- **Date:** When captured

---

## Step 4: Scale — Build the Flywheel

```
AI Generates → Expert Rejects → Rejection Encoded → Library Grows → AI Output Improves → Repeat
```

- Each cycle raises the quality bar
- Juniors can access senior taste via the constraint library
- The encoded residue of expert judgment persists across projects and personnel changes

---

## Key Takeaway

> Your competitive moat is not which AI vendor you choose (models are commoditizing). It is the depth and durability of your organization's encoded taste. Stop letting rejections evaporate in Slack threads — capture them as structured, reusable constraints.
