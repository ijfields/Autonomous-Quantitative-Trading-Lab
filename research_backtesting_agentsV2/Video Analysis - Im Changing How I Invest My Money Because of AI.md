## KEY TOPICS

### S&P 500 Concentration Risk
- 40 cents of every dollar → just 10 companies (Nvidia, Microsoft, Apple, Alphabet, Amazon, Broadcom, Meta, Tesla, Berkshire, JP Morgan)
- Nearly all making massive AI bets with future revenue expectations, not current earnings
- All AI companies combined would need ~$2 trillion in AI revenue to justify current valuations
- Deutsche Bank: US economy would already be in recession without AI spending
- Dangerous feedback loop: larger companies → more passive investment → higher prices → larger S&P share → more passive money

### 4-Quadrant Market Framework

| Quadrant | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Crowded Zone | Large cap, expensive | Nvidia, Tesla, Meta |
| Defensive Zone | Large cap, reasonable value | McDonald's, Walmart, Coca-Cola |
| Speculative Zone | Small cap, hyped | Beyond Meat, Peloton, AMC |
| Overlooked Zone | Small/mid cap, undervalued AI adopters | Companies that USE AI, not BUILD it |

**Thesis:** AI models are commoditizing (users can switch Claude/ChatGPT/Gemini), leading to price war hurting builders but benefiting smart adopters.

### Gold Allocation Increase
- China dramatically increased reserves since 2023
- Gold overtook US Treasuries as world's largest foreign reserve asset (first time since 1996)
- Gold reclassified as Basel III Tier 1 asset (treated same as cash on bank balance sheets)
- Bank of America: 30% reserves in gold (up from 20%)
- Tilbury buys physical gold + DCA into iShares Physical Gold ETF via Trading 212

## ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

1. Reduce S&P 500 concentration by diversifying globally (VWRP or FSPSX)
2. Invest in small/mid-cap companies that *adopt* AI rather than *build* it
3. Increase gold allocation (physical + ETF)
4. Hold more cash for crash protection and buying opportunities
5. Don't try to time the market — stay invested but diversify
6. AI model commoditization means value flows to adopters, not builders
