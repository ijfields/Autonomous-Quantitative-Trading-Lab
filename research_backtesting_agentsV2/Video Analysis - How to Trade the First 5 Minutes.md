## KEY TOPICS

### The Opening Problem

- First 15 minutes of trading = noisiest part of the day
- Market is in "discovery" mode — filling overnight orders, pricing in news, early positioning
- Most traders take their worst trades in this period because they're guessing direction
- Losing trades at the open set up a bad psychological cascade for the rest of the day

### The Fence Bar Setup

#### Step 1: Draw the Fence (9:35 ET)
- **Chart:** 5-minute QQQ
- **Action:** Mark the high and low of the first 5-minute candle (9:30-9:35)
- High = **top rail** of the fence
- Low = **bottom rail** of the fence
- These two levels capture all the opening discovery activity
- **Insight:** "That first candle captured everything the market was sorting out at the open"

#### Step 2: Wait for Close Outside the Fence
- Watch subsequent 5-minute candles
- **Trigger:** A candle that **closes completely outside** the fence (not just wicks)
- If candle closes above top rail → potential long setup
- If candle closes below bottom rail → potential short setup
- Wicks that poke outside but close inside do NOT count

#### Step 3: Wait for the Retest
- After the breakout candle, wait for the next candle to **wick back into the fence area** and then **close back outside**
- This is the retest — price is confirming the breakout level
- Most traders panic during this retest thinking the breakout failed
- **Entry point:** Close of the retest candle

#### Step 4: Set Stop-Loss
- **Rule of thumb:** About 50% into the fence from the breakout side
- **Better rule:** Place stop at nearest clear support/resistance level within the fence
- If the fence is very large, don't use the full 50% — find a logical level
- For longs: stop just below visible support inside the fence
- For shorts: stop just above visible resistance inside the fence

#### Step 5: Take Profit
- **Target:** 2x risk (2R)
- Measure the distance from entry to stop, copy it twice above/below entry
- If 2R target seems far and price is extended from the 20 SMA, be ready to take less
- First target is the priority; can hold for more if setup looks strong

### The Anchor Line (20 SMA)

- **What:** 20-period simple moving average on the 5-minute chart
- **Purpose:** Shows when the market is "in balance" — directional context
- **Rules:**
  - Anchor moving WITH your trade direction → full confidence, hold for 2R
  - Anchor flat → acceptable, proceed normally
  - Anchor moving AGAINST your trade direction → reduce size or skip
- **Far from anchor:** Increased probability of a "snapback" (mean reversion to the 20)
  - Lower expectations for profit target
  - Be quicker to lock in gains near 2R
  - If hairpin turn occurs, exit for ~1R instead
- **Near anchor:** More patience allowed; riding the trend is safer

### When It Doesn't Work

- **No retest:** Price breaks out and keeps going without coming back to test the fence
- This is the "Achilles heel" — you miss the move entirely
- **Not a failure** — you simply don't have a trade, which protects capital

### Relationship to Opening Power Bar Strategy

- Stephen's primary strategy is the "Opening Power Bar" on a 2-minute chart
- Opening Power Bar doesn't fire every day
- Fence Bar is the **backup strategy** for days when the primary doesn't trigger
- Can be used independently on the 5-minute chart
- Power Bar entries would have been slightly earlier (at the breakout, not the retest)

## ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

1. **Mark the first 5-min candle at 9:35** — high and low become your fence rails; that's it for setup
2. **Wait for a close outside, not just a wick** — the candle body must finish outside the fence
3. **The retest is the entry** — don't chase the breakout; wait for price to come back and test the fence, then close outside again
4. **Stop at 50% into the fence** (or nearest S/R level) — keeps risk defined and reasonable
5. **Target 2x your risk** — simple, consistent profit-taking rule
6. **Use the 20 SMA as context** — trade direction should align with the anchor; reduce expectations when far from it
7. **No retest = no trade** — the strategy's weakness is also its safety: you only enter confirmed moves
8. **Works as backup to ORB strategies** — use on days when your primary opening strategy doesn't trigger
