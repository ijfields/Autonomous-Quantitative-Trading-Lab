## Step 1: Identify the Geopolitical Event

1. Monitor news sources for escalating conflicts (wars, sanctions, supply disruptions)
2. Identify which commodity markets are directly affected:
   - **Oil** — supply disruptions, Middle East conflicts
   - **Gold** — safe haven demand
   - **Natural gas** — energy supply chain impacts
   - **BTC** — risk-on/risk-off sentiment shifts

---

## Step 2: Check Hyperliquid HIP3 Positioning

1. Go to Hyperliquid and navigate to HIP3 traditional market instruments
2. Look at the long/short ratio for affected commodities
3. **Key signal:** Extreme positioning (>70% one direction) = contrarian opportunity

| Positioning | Signal |
|------------|--------|
| >70% short | Potential short squeeze — consider long |
| >70% long | Potential long squeeze — consider short |
| 50/50 | No positioning edge — look elsewhere |

4. Check liquidation data (unique to Hyperliquid — not available on traditional exchanges)
5. Identify liquidation clusters that could cascade

---

## Step 3: Assess Probability with Polymarket

1. Navigate to Polymarket
2. Search for relevant prediction markets (ceasefire odds, escalation, etc.)
3. Use the probability as a quantified assessment:

| Ceasefire Probability | Interpretation |
|----------------------|----------------|
| <30% | Conflict likely continues — war trades have runway |
| 30-50% | Uncertain — smaller position sizes |
| >50% | De-escalation likely — consider taking profits |

4. Consider "no" side bets as their own trade if you have conviction

## Step 6: Identify the Lagging Trade

Not all commodities move simultaneously during geopolitical events:

1. **First movers:** Oil and gold (react within hours)
2. **Lagging movers:** Natural gas, agricultural commodities (react over days/weeks)
3. **Recovery plays:** BTC and equities (sell off first, recover as market digests)

The lagging trade often offers the best risk/reward — the event is known but the price hasn't moved yet.
