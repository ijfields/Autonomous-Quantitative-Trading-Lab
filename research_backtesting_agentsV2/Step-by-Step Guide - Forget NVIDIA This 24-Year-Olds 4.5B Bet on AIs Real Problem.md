## Step 3: Understand the New Positions

### Power Generation — Bloom Energy (#1 Position, $855M)

**Why Bloom Energy:**
- Solid oxide fuel cells convert natural gas to electricity **on-site**
- No dependence on the electrical grid (which is constrained by human demand)
- Modular — can be deployed quickly next to any data center
- 2 gigawatts of capacity planned for this year
- Revenue: +34% in 2025, projecting +40% in 2026
- $20 billion demand backlog
- 20% of Leopold's entire portfolio

**Investment thesis:** Bloom could be the "NVIDIA of energy" — the dominant provider of on-site power generation for AI data centers.

### Compute & Hosting — CoreWeave (#2 Position, $800M)

**Why CoreWeave:**
- NeoCloud: handles GPU rack setup, cooling, electrical engineering for AI labs
- AI labs need GPUs, but setting them up requires massive infrastructure expertise
- $500M added in Q3, another $300M this quarter (700% increase)
- Leopold also owns ~10% of Core Scientific (CoreWeave's energy grid supplier)

### Real Estate & Permits — Bitcoin Mining Companies (10+ new positions)

**Why Bitcoin miners:**
- They already own the two hardest-to-get assets for AI data centers:
  1. **Real estate** (large facilities with space for GPU racks)
  2. **Electrical grid permits** (takes months/years to obtain normally)
- Leopold is NOT interested in mining crypto
- Strategy: acquire → gut crypto operations → repurpose for AI training/inference
- "Like taking over a bar that already has a liquor license"

### Short Position — Infosys

**Why short Infosys:**
- Indian IT outsourcing company (cheap labor model)
- Claude Code, GPT Codex 5.3 can now automate the IT work Infosys charges for
- If AI coding tools replace outsourced labor, Infosys's business model breaks

## Step 5: Apply the Framework

The generalizable lesson from Leopold's approach:

1. **Identify the current bottleneck** in any major technology buildout
2. **Invest before the market prices it in** — when GPUs were underpriced, he bought GPU stocks; now energy is underpriced
3. **Exit when the market catches up** — NVIDIA is now fully priced; sell and rotate
4. **Look for non-obvious infrastructure plays** — Bitcoin miners for permits, not crypto
5. **Read primary sources** — Leopold's 165-page essay is his investment bible; the best investors write their thesis first
6. **High conviction, concentrated bets** — Leopold doesn't diversify for safety; he concentrates where he has edge
