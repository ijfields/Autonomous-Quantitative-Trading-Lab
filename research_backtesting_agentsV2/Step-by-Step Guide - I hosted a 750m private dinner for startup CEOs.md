# Step-by-Step Guide: Hosting Elite Founder Dinners (Jeffersonian Style)

**Source:** Brandon Bryant / Harlem Capital (YouTube)
**Video ID:** utT59tlMzmw
**Upload Date:** 2026-03-02

---

## What This Guide Covers

How to host relationship-building dinners for founders and investors using the Jeffersonian format — one conversation, strategic seating, and intentional community building.

---

## Step 1: Choose a Co-Host

- Partner with a well-connected founder or investor
- They bring their peer network (2nd/3rd degree connections)
- Choose someone whose network complements yours

---

## Step 2: Curate the Guest List

- 20+ founders/operators
- Look for "A players" — they attract other A players
- Match sponsors with potential customers
- Find similarities between attendees (industry, background, interests)

---

## Step 3: Plan Strategic Seating

- Place people with complementary needs near each other
- If sponsors are present, seat them next to potential value matches
- Look for any dot of connection → there are usually many more

---

## Step 4: Run the Jeffersonian Format

1. One conversation for the whole table (no side conversations)
2. Cold-call every person — everyone speaks
3. Prepare backup questions in case conversation stalls
4. Keep it off-the-record for candid discussion
5. Topics: AI, fundraising, hiring, industry trends

---

## Step 5: Follow Up

- Follow up with all attendees after the event
- Invite them to future programming
- Make introductions between people who hit it off
- Track relationship development over time

---

## Reference Checking Framework

When evaluating founders or hires, ask three questions:

| # | Question | What It Reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Is this person exceptional? | Baseline quality |
| 2 | Would you work for them? | Leadership/trust |
| 3 | Are you investing personal capital? | Skin in the game |

**Speed signal:** How fast references respond indicates relationship strength.

---

## Key Principles

- Meet people where they are (workouts, karaoke, sports, dinners)
- Play the long game (8-year sales cycles are real)
- Building community IS deal flow
- Genuine relationships > transactional networking
