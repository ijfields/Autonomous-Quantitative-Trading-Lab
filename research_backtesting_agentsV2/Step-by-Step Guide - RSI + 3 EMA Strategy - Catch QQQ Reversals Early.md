## Chart Setup

### Step 1: Add Three EMAs to Daily Chart
- **5 EMA** (hot pink) — fastest, first to react
- **11 EMA** (red) — medium speed, confirms direction
- **18 EMA** (blue) — slowest, defines the primary trend

### Step 2: Add RSI(11)
- Set RSI to **11 periods** (not standard 14) for faster turns
- Mark overbought zone above **80**
- Mark oversold zone below **25**

---

## Selling / Exiting Long Positions

### Step 3: Watch for Air Gap Warning
When price extends significantly above the 18 EMA, a large "air gap" forms. This signals elevated pullback risk — consider tightening stops or taking partial profits.

### Step 4: Identify Bearish RSI Divergence (Sell Signal)
- Price is making higher highs
- RSI(11) is making lower highs
- This is a **sell signal** — warning only, not yet a trigger

### Step 5: Wait for Sell Trigger
- Price breaks a short-term uptrend line
- 5 and 11 EMAs curl downward
- Combined with the divergence signal = **sell trigger** → exit to cash

---

## Buying / Entering Long Positions

### Step 6: Wait for EMA Crossover (Buy Signal)
- 5 EMA crosses above 11 EMA
- Both cross above 18 EMA
- All three EMAs pointing northeast

### Step 7: Confirm with RSI
- RSI(11) breaks above recent peaks (above 60-65 zone)
- RSI rising in same direction as price = genuine bullish momentum
- If RSI is overbought (>80), that is **bullish**, not a sell signal

### Step 8: Choose Entry Method
- **Aggressive:** Enter at the crossover candle
- **Conservative:** Wait for the 1-2-3 pullback pattern:
  1. Initial move up
  2. Pullback to test support (look for hammer candle)
  3. Continuation — enter on the bounce

### Step 9: Set Stop-Loss
- Place stop below the recent swing low
- Example: Entry at 409, stop at 390-392

### Step 10: Manage the Position
- Trail stops upward as price advances — move to just below the most recent significant candle
- Example: Raise stop from 392 to 403 as price moves from 409 to 423
- Exit when sell trigger fires (Step 5)

## Common Pitfalls
- **Entering during an air gap:** Don't buy when price is far above the 18 EMA — risk of pullback is too high
- **Confusing overbought RSI with a sell signal:** RSI above 80 means strong bullish momentum, not "time to sell"
- **Trading on signals without triggers:** Divergence warns; uptrend line break confirms — wait for both
- **Ignoring the 18 EMA direction:** If the 18 EMA is still pointing up during a short-term dip, the larger trend is bullish
- **RSI in the middle zone (40-60):** RSI works best at extremes; middle readings provide little useful information
