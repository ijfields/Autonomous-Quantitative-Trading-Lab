## Overview
A focused tutorial presenting two RSI-based strategies that use RSI set to 21 periods (instead of the standard 14) combined with price action. The speaker argues that RSI(14) produces too many false signals, that overbought/oversold zones alone are unreliable, and that the 50% zone is the most important RSI level. Both strategies require price action confirmation before entry.

---

## Key Concepts

### RSI Settings
- **RSI period: 21** (not standard 14) — produces fewer false signals based on the speaker's years of market observation
- **50% zone is the most critical level**: above 50 = bullish, below 50 = bearish
- **Overbought/oversold zones are NOT entry signals** — the market can stay oversold and keep dropping

### Divergence
- Price making higher highs while RSI makes lower highs = bearish divergence
- Price making lower lows while RSI makes higher lows = bullish divergence
- Divergence is a signal, NOT a trigger — requires confirmation

### Price Action Confirmation
- Support/resistance breakouts validate divergence signals
- Order blocks (origin of significant moves) serve as targets
- Confirmation candles at key levels provide entry timing

## Backtestable Parameters

### Strategy 1: RSI Divergence Reversal
- **RSI:** 21-period
- **Setup:** Bearish divergence — price higher highs, RSI lower highs
- **Confirmation:** Price breaks below support zone AND RSI breaks below 50
- **Entry:** On confirmed breakdown
- **Target:** Previous order block / price zone (origin of prior bullish move)
- **Stop-loss:** Slightly above the broken support zone
- **Direction:** Short (bearish divergence) or Long (bullish divergence — inverse logic)

### Strategy 2: RSI 50% Bounce Trend Following
- **RSI:** 21-period
- **Setup:** Established uptrend, price pulls back to former resistance (now support)
- **Confirmation:** Bullish confirmation candle at support + RSI bounces off 50% zone
- **Optional confluence:** Fibonacci 61.8% retracement aligns with support zone
- **Entry:** At close of 15-minute confirmation candle
- **Stop-loss:** Just below the confirmation candle / price zone
- **Target:** Minimum 2:1 risk/reward (e.g., $50 risk → $100 target)
- **Continuation:** When price breaks prior high, take additional long position

## Actionable Takeaways
1. Switch RSI from 14 to 21 periods for fewer false signals
2. Never buy just because RSI is oversold — the market can stay oversold and keep dropping
3. The 50% RSI level is the key dividing line between bullish and bearish conditions
4. Always require price action confirmation (breakout or confirmation candle) before entering on a divergence
5. Use order blocks as targets — they represent levels where institutional buying/selling originated
6. Stack multiple confluences (divergence + support/resistance + Fibonacci + RSI 50 bounce) for highest-probability entries
