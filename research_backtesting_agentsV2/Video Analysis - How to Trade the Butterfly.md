## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Seth Pearlberg from SMB Capital walks through the Iron Butterfly as the core options income strategy of their trading desk. A live SPX example: sell 10x ATM 2815 calls and puts, buy 10x 2840 calls and 2790 puts (25-point wings), collecting $19,400 credit against $5,600 margin. Held to expiration, the trade netted $15,300 — a ~3:1 risk/reward. Butterflies can be structured for up to 10:1 ratios.

## KEY TOPICS

### Iron Butterfly Structure

- **Short** ATM call + short ATM put (same strike)
- **Long** OTM call above + long OTM put below (equidistant wings)
- Constructed using **SPX index options** (cash-settled, $100 per point)

### Live Trade Example

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Underlying** | SPX at 2815 |
| **Short strikes** | 10x 2815 calls + 10x 2815 puts (ATM) |
| **Long wings** | 10x 2840 calls + 10x 2790 puts (25-point width) |
| **Credit received** | $19,400 |
| **Margin (max loss)** | $5,600 |
| **Risk/reward** | ~3:1 (can be structured up to 10:1) |
| **SPX at expiry** | 2810.90 |
| **Settlement cost** | $4,100 (short 2815 put $4.10 ITM) |
| **Net profit** | $15,300 |

### How Settlement Worked

- Both calls (2815, 2840) expired worthless (above closing price)
- Long 2790 put expired worthless (below closing price)
- Short 2815 put was $4.10 ITM → cost $4,100 to settle
- Retained $15,300 of $19,400 credit

## ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS

1. **Iron Butterfly provides large upfront credit** with capped, known max risk
2. **Risk/reward ranges from 3:1 to 10:1** depending on structure
3. **SPX cash settlement** means no physical shares — receive $100 per point of intrinsic value
4. **Professional traders exit before expiration** rather than holding to settlement
5. **Not every trade will be this successful** — this was an ideal outcome
6. **Structure can be modified mid-trade** to adapt to changing prices and conditions
