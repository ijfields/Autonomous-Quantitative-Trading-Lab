## Step 1: Set Up Your AI Development Environment

1. Install **VS Code** as your primary IDE
2. Install **Kilo AI** extension (free tier available, 500+ cloud models)
3. Use Architecture Mode for asking questions about trading concepts, interview prep, and system design
4. Prefer cloud-based AI models over local — they update constantly

---

## Step 2: Choose Your Trading Infrastructure

### For Equities
- **Interactive Brokers (IB)** — low commission, automation-friendly, good API
- Paper trading available for testing
- Customer service scales with account size ($200K+ gets US-based support)

### For Futures
- **Rithmic** — better feed quality and responsiveness than IB
- API costs: ~$140/month (requires exchange permissions)
- **Edgeclear** — recommended futures broker (personal service)

## Step 4: Prepare for Quant Interviews

1. **Format has changed**: Firms now use role-playing scenarios, not static questions
2. Practice with AI — role-play interview scenarios with Claude/Kilo AI
3. Focus on **futures and options** knowledge (especially options)
4. **Skills hierarchy**: Problem-solving > AI proficiency > domain knowledge > coding
5. LeetCode is useful for building challenge-overcoming mindset but questions won't be reused
6. Most positions require minimum Master's degree

---

## Step 5: Build Your First Trading Bot

1. Start with Python as your primary language
2. Use hftcode.com blueprint for IB + Python bot templates (~7 templates)
3. Begin with paper trading on Interactive Brokers
4. Use AI (Claude, Kilo AI) to generate and iterate on bot code
5. Watch for **DeepSeek 4** release — from a quant shop, expected strong for math/quant work

## Step 6: Scale from Practitioner to Entrepreneur

1. Algo trading has low capital outlay compared to traditional businesses
2. The future is managing 20-100+ AI agents as a systematic portfolio manager
3. 30% of hedge fund trading is already agent-driven
4. Single-person AI-powered trading operations are viable ($15-20M exits possible)
