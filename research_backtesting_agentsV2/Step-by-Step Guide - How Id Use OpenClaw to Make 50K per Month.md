## What This Guide Covers

A three-tier business model for turning the free, open-source OpenClaw AI agent platform into a consulting revenue stream, from simple done-for-you setups to premium multi-agent operational transformations.

---

## Step 1: Understand the Business Opportunity

1. OpenClaw is a free open-source AI agent with 200K+ GitHub stars and 5,000+ pluggable skills.
2. It can read emails, monitor Slack, manage files, run scheduled tasks, control browsers, and connect 50+ tools.
3. The money is in the **gap**: OpenClaw requires command-line knowledge, Node.js, API keys, security hardening, soul files, and agents files -- things 95% of business owners cannot handle.
4. Your role is to bridge that gap by understanding business problems and deploying the tool to solve them.

---

## Step 2: Level 1 -- Done-for-You OpenClaw Setup ($500-$5,000)

1. Get on a 30-minute discovery call with a founder or executive.
2. Ask three questions:
   - What tools do you use every day?
   - What eats up most of your time?
   - What do you wish just happened automatically?
3. Set up OpenClaw on their server or machine.
4. Configure the skills they need.
5. Write their **soul file** (defines the agent's personality and values) and **agents file** (defines operating rules and boundaries).
6. Connect their email, Slack, calendar, and other tools.
7. Test the system and hand it over with a user guide.
8. Charge $500-$1,000 for initial clients while building proof of results, then scale to $5,000.
9. Ongoing: budget approximately 2 hours per month for maintenance.

---

## Step 3: Level 2 -- Recurring AI Systems (Monthly Retainer)

1. Instead of a one-time setup, build an ongoing AI system that runs a part of the client's business.
2. **Case study (Stewardly -- yacht management):**
   - Problem: Chief stewardess spent 10 hours per trip counting inventory by hand using sticky notes and notebooks. Crew waited 30 min to 2 hours for stock answers.
   - Solution: WhatsApp-based AI agent. Crew texts items (e.g., "2 beach towels, 1 sunscreen"), system categorizes, updates database in real time, confirms changes. Supports voice notes and multiple languages.
   - Results: 10 hours dropped to under 1 hour per trip. Projected $21K/year in labor savings, $19K in supply savings. Stock checks in under 2 minutes.
3. This is a recurring revenue client: build once, manage monthly, collect payment every month.

---

## Step 4: Level 3 -- Full Operational Audit ($10,000-$20,000)

1. Walk into a company and audit their entire operation.
2. Find every workflow that consumes human hours.
3. Map out which problems AI can solve and which it cannot.
4. Design a multi-agent system to transform operations.
5. **Case study (Flight Freun -- youth sports academy):**
   - Problem: Over-complicated CRM (Glowfox), manual reassignment of kids between age groups, 4-person support team buried in WhatsApp messages all day.
   - Solution: Custom CRM for their niche, WhatsApp integration for 24/7 automated responses, voice agent handling phone inquiries in multiple languages, staff training agent for new coaches.
   - Pricing: Three packages from $10,000 to $20,000.
6. The premium pricing comes from the rare skill of understanding a business deeply enough to transform it, not from the technology being harder.

---

## Step 5: Handle Limitations Honestly

1. **Security:** You are giving an AI agent access to email, files, CRM, calendar. Have an explicit conversation about risk, trust, and guard rails with every client.
2. **Mistakes:** Skills break on updates, agents hallucinate, and they sometimes do things unprompted. Set proper soul file boundaries.
3. **Monitoring:** Plan for ongoing maintenance. Your job is to understand limitations better than the client and to set the guard rails.
4. Position yourself as the long-term mechanic, not just the salesperson.

---

## Step 6: Get Your First Client This Week

1. Find one person you know who runs a business (friend, LinkedIn connection, local business).
2. Ask: "What takes up most of your time that you wish just happened on its own?"
3. Listen to their answer -- that is your first project.
4. You do not need a perfect OpenClaw setup, a website, or another tutorial. You need one real conversation with one real business owner.
5. The first client will not be perfect, but it will teach you more in one week than six months of tutorials.

---

## Key Takeaway

> The tool is free. The gap between the tool and what a business actually needs -- that is your business. Focus on understanding business problems and applying AI to solve them, and you will never run out of work regardless of which new tool drops next.
