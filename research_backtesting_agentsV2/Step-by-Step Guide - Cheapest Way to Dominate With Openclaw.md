## What This Guide Covers

The most cost-effective hardware and software setup for running OpenClaw productively, based on $10,000+ worth of experimentation with Mac Studios, Mac Minis, gaming PCs, and local LLMs.

---

## The Recommendation

**Hardware:** 1x cheapest Mac Mini (~$600)
**Software:** Claude Max subscription ($200/month)

---

## Step 1: Get a Mac Mini (Base Model)

| Option | Price |
|--------|-------|
| Apple Store | ~$600 |
| Student discount | ~$500 |
| Micro Center | ~$400 |

- Do NOT buy a Mac Studio ($4,000-$10,000)
- Do NOT buy an upgraded Mac Mini ($2,000-$3,000)
- The base model is sufficient for running OpenClaw with cloud-based models

---

## Step 2: Subscribe to Claude Max

- $200/month for Opus 4.6 access
- Significantly smarter and faster than any local model
- Never hit usage limits (per Ashen's experience)
- Can cancel anytime — no hardware depreciation risk

---

## Step 3: Skip Local LLMs (Unless You Need Privacy/Free/24x7)

Only run local models if you specifically need:
1. **Privacy** — no data sent to cloud
2. **Free inference** — no ongoing API costs
3. **24/7 uptime** — always available

If those three don't matter to you, cloud subscriptions are better in every way.

---

## Cost Comparison

| Setup | Upfront | Monthly | 1-Year Total |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------|
| Mac Mini + Claude Max | $600 | $200 | $3,000 |
| Mac Studio + Local LLM | $5,000-$10,000 | $0 | $5,000-$10,000 |

Break-even: 2-4+ years before the Mac Studio setup becomes cheaper, and by then hardware is outdated.

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't buy expensive hardware for FOMO
- Don't run Qwen/Ollama on basic hardware — too slow, too dumb
- Don't expect local 7B-17B models to match Opus 4.6 quality
- Apple has a **1-week return policy** — use it to test before committing
