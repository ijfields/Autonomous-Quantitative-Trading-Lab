## KEY TOPICS

### The 6-Step Framework

#### Step 1: Stop Throwing Bodies at Problems
- Old model: more revenue → more employees → more complexity
- New model: identify bottleneck → automate with AI → complexity shrinks as revenue grows
- "Your job is to design the system that does the work"

#### Step 2: Find a Painful Problem Worth Solving
- Fall in love with the customer's problem, not the tech
- Look for "painkiller" problems in growing markets (AI, automation, healthcare, real estate, coaching)
- Call people for advice (not to sell)
- Talk to at least 10 people to co-design initial specs

#### Step 3: Solve the Problem Manually First
- Fix it by hand before automating — get paid to learn the steps
- Example: Matt built "Precision" — started with a spreadsheet before building the automated AI platform
- Draft a one-page offer: Problem, Promise, Timeline, Price, Guarantee
- Call back the 10 people and present the offer

#### Step 4: Build a Clickable Prototype (Wizard of Oz)
- Don't spend $50K building a product
- Build a fake-but-realistic prototype using Figma, UX Pilot.ai, or Visily.ai
- Test with 5 new customers — record reactions, watch what they click
- "You'll learn more in five customer calls than in five weeks of coding"

#### Step 5: Build Your MVP
- Core features only — Facebook started with one college, Amazon with just books
- Demonstrates using Manis AI to generate a full-stack app from a single prompt
- Prompt template: core promise, 3 screens (login, data input, output/insights), authentication, clean/minimal UI, no extra features
- Iterate by talking to Manis like a person

#### Step 6: Scale with AI Agents, Not Headcount
Three stages:
| Revenue | Model |
|---------|-------|
| $0-$100K | You do everything, AI helps you move faster |
| $100K-$1M | Build systems AI can run (onboarding, support, operations) |
| $1M-$10M | Stack AI agents and workflows, only loop yourself in for decisions |

- Real example: Martell Ventures company doing $83K MRR with 1 founder + 2 part-time contractors

### Key Business Principles
- "Complexity kills more businesses than competition"
- The metric to brag about is revenue-per-person, not team size
- Crowdfunding and consulting prove demand before building
- Resist feature requests unless they impact 80% of users

## KEY TAKEAWAY

> The biggest mistake AI founders make is falling in love with the tech instead of the customer's problem. The framework: (1) find a painful problem, (2) solve it manually and get paid, (3) build a clickable prototype to test with real customers, (4) build an MVP with core features only, (5) scale with AI agents instead of employees. A real Martell Ventures company proves it works: $83K MRR with just 1 founder + 2 part-time contractors, running entirely on AI workflows.
