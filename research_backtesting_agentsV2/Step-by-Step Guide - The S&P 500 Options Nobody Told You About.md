## What This Guide Covers

How to trade ES futures options as a capital-efficient alternative to SPY/SPX options, including iron condors, gold-silver ratio trades, and covered call spreads using micro futures.

---

## Prerequisites

- Futures-approved brokerage account (e.g., tastytrade)
- Understanding of options Greeks (delta, theta, vega)
- Familiarity with basic options strategies (spreads, iron condors)

---

## Step 1: Understand the Key Differences

| Adjustment | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Multiplier** | $50/point (not $100). A "100" price = $5,000 |
| **Margin** | SPAN (buying power often less than max loss) |
| **Settlement** | Deliverable — ITM options become futures positions |
| **Expirations** | Daily (Mon-Fri), liquid across the curve |
| **Style** | European (no early exercise), except quarterly AM options |

---

## Step 2: Trade an ES Iron Condor

1. Choose expiration 30-60 DTE
2. Sell put spread and call spread at ~20 delta each side
3. Use 100-point width (or adjust to your risk tolerance)
4. Example: Short 7100/7200 call spread + Short 6300/6200 put spread
5. Collect ~40% of width as credit (SPAN-margined)
6. Manage at 50% of max profit or 21 DTE, whichever comes first

---

## Step 3: Trade a Gold-Silver Ratio Pair

1. Check gold-silver ratio (gold price / silver price)
2. At ~58+ ratio: long gold / short silver (silver relatively cheap)
3. Use micro contracts: 1 oz gold (1OZ) vs 1 SIC (micro silver) at 1:1
4. Alternative sizing: 1 MGC vs 8 SIC
5. Mean-reversion trade — close when ratio normalizes

---

## Step 4: Build a Micro NQ Covered Call Spread

1. Buy 2 MNQ (micro NASDAQ) futures (~$50K notional each)
2. Sell 1 NQ call spread ~50 DTE at ~25% of width
3. Delta math: 2 MNQ = ~20 NQ deltas; short spread reduces by ~4 delta
4. Net: ~15-16 NQ deltas with theta decay and short vol component
5. SPAN cross-margins the positions for capital efficiency

---

## Key Warnings

- **Deliverability:** Close or roll positions before expiration to avoid unwanted futures positions
- **AM quarterly options (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec):** American-style, last trading day is THURSDAY
- **Multiplier math:** Always multiply by $50, not $100

---

## Key Takeaway

> ES futures options give you the same S&P 500 exposure as SPY/SPX with better margin treatment, daily expirations, and flexible contract sizing via micros. The SPAN margin system makes defined-risk trades significantly more capital-efficient than Reg-T.
