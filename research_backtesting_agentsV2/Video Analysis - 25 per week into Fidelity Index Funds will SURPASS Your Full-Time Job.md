### Psychology of Early Investing

- **Why people quit:** Early results feel discouraging — balance barely moves
- **Linear thinking trap:** We expect effort → reward, but investing is exponential (delayed payoff)
- **Market noise:** Down weeks feel like failure when balance is small
- **Solution:** Change the success metric from "How much did it grow?" to **"Did I invest this week?"**
- **Key insight:** Remove emotional ups and downs by focusing on the controllable action (investing), not the uncontrollable outcome (returns)

### Fund Selection Criteria

Five key qualities for long-term investing:
1. **Diversification** — Own hundreds/thousands of companies, not single stocks
2. **Broad exposure** — Mix of large, mid, small, international; don't guess which segment wins
3. **Dividend growth** — Growing dividends buy more shares → more dividends → compounding cycle
4. **Proven performance** — Funds that survived multiple market cycles (booms, crashes, recessions)
5. **Low costs** — Even 1% fees significantly reduce long-term results; zero/minimal expense ratios
