## What This Guide Covers

20 distilled business lessons from 13 years of entrepreneurship: pricing, sales, hiring, customer research, and scaling frameworks.

---

## Step 1: Design Your Grand Slam Offer

1. Stack bonuses, guarantees, and urgency until refusal is irrational
2. Add a 10x-100x premium option you don't expect to sell (price anchoring)
3. Revenue = leads × conversion rate × price × number of purchases — optimize all four

---

## Step 2: Get Your First Customers

1. Use the **100 Person Method**: reach out to 100 people you know
2. Do free work first to collect proof and testimonials
3. Warm outreach via phone/text/DM — don't start with paid ads

---

## Step 3: Build Proof

1. Collect testimonials at the moment of greatest customer satisfaction
2. Prioritize: recent > old, visual > text, high volume > low volume
3. Start proof collection with pain ("before" stories convert higher)

---

## Step 4: Raise Your Prices

1. If you haven't raised prices since 2017, inflation has erased ~20% of margin
2. Test 2-4x price increases — even with 35% fewer conversions, you can triple profit
3. Grandfather existing customers with a price increase letter
4. Raise by at least inflation (3-6%) annually

---

## Step 5: Use the CLOSER Sales Framework

1. **C** — Clarify why they're there (what made them respond)
2. **L** — Label the problem ("So it sounds like...")
3. **O** — Overview past experiences (temporarily increase deprivation)
4. **S** — Sell the vacation, not the plane flight (outcomes, not features)
5. **E** — Explain away concerns (specifics, time, money, decision-maker)
6. **R** — Reinforce the decision (overdeliver in first 24 hours)

---

## Step 6: Scale What Works (10x Rule)

1. Before trying new channels, ask: "Why can't we 10x what already works?"
2. Two-thirds of the time, the answer is "we could"
3. Avoid the entrepreneur doom loop: uninformed optimism → informed pessimism → valley of despair → restart with new idea

---

## Step 7: Optimize Billing and Retention

1. Customers judge purchases by the most recent billing cycle
2. Bill less frequently (quarterly/annually) to extend the look-back window
3. Even 10% of customers on annual plans = 12x upfront cash flow
4. Add guarantees to prepaid plans to offset customer risk

---

## Step 8: Monetize Excess Capacity

1. Look at assets, talent, and infrastructure you already have
2. Repackage as additional revenue without operational drag
3. Examples: rent gym space mid-day, use team knowledge for workshops
4. "Double dip" by using the same activity for multiple outputs

---

## Step 9: Build a Sales Content Arsenal

1. Create an indexed library of content addressing every specific objection
2. Sales reps send relevant content between calls
3. Outsource selling to content — reduces training burden

---

## Step 10: Hire and Lead Effectively

1. Every new hire must raise the team average (Bezos rule)
2. Interview 10-20 candidates to calibrate
3. The person with lowest tolerance for mediocrity should lead
4. Five reasons someone underperforms: didn't know you wanted it / didn't know how / didn't know when / didn't want to / something blocking them

---

## Key Takeaway

> Identify the single hardest priority that, if solved, makes other goals irrelevant. Most entrepreneurs avoid this work because it requires confronting the unknown — and instead keep solving familiar problems. The bigger the rock, the bigger the bag of money.
