## What This Guide Covers

How to buy a building for your small business with zero down payment using the SBA 7(a) loan program — the requirements, qualifications, and how to find lenders who offer it.

---

## Step 1: Understand the SBA 7(a) Reality

- Most banks say 10% down is required — **this is wrong**
- The SBA's actual rule: assess the business's equity position and determine if injection is needed
- No standard 10% requirement exists in the SBA SOP
- 100% financing IS allowed under SBA 7(a)

---

## Step 2: Assess Your Balance Sheet

Your balance sheet determines eligibility:

| Strong Balance Sheet (Likely Qualifies) | Weak Balance Sheet (Likely Needs Down Payment) |
|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Many years in business | New/young business |
| Low debt in the business | Overly leveraged |
| Retained earnings (don't drain profits) | Treat business like a piggy bank |

---

## Step 3: Compare Your Options

| Financing Option | Down Payment | Notes |
|-----------------|-------------|-------|
| Conventional bank | 20% + closing | Standard commercial mortgage |
| SBA 504 | 10% | Common SBA option |
| SBA 7(a) — standard bank | 10% | Bank's rule, not SBA's |
| SBA 7(a) — informed bank | 0% possible | Based on balance sheet strength |

---

## Step 4: Find the Right Lender

- Not every bank offers 100% financing
- "A couple dozen" currently do (and growing)
- Look for SBA lenders who understand the actual SOP rules
- Ask specifically: "Do you offer SBA 7(a) with no equity injection for strong balance sheets?"

---

## Step 5: Consider the All-in-One Loan

The SBA 7(a) can bundle:
- Real estate purchase
- Equipment financing
- Inventory
- Working capital

All in one loan — no need for separate financing.

---

## Why Own vs. Lease

| Own | Lease |
|-----|-------|
| Build equity for yourself | Build equity for landlord |
| No lease renewal uncertainty | Renegotiate every 5 years |
| Sell business, keep building | Lose location when lease ends |
| Passive income as future landlord | Nothing when you retire |
