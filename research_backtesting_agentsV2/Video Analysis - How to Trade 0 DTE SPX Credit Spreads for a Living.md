## Key Concepts

### The MAX(S) Framework
1. **M — Market Trend:** Use daily chart for long-term trend, 30-minute chart for short-term trend. Align trades with the dominant direction.
2. **A — Area of Interest:** Draw horizontal support/resistance at swing highs and lows. Confirm with implied volatility — good areas show IV spikes when price enters.
3. **C — Criteria of Entry:** Never enter just because price reaches an area. Require candlestick confirmation:
   - Bullish: Hammer at support → next candle breaks above hammer's high
   - Bearish: Candle closes below support → breakdown confirmed
4. **S — Stop-Loss:** Set stop slightly beyond the confirming candle formation. If thesis invalidated, exit immediately.

### Trade Structure
- 5-point wide SPX put credit spreads (or call credit spreads for bearish)
- Entered at or near the money at support/resistance levels
- Risk/reward approximately 1:3 ($50 loss per spread vs. $155 gain)
- 0DTE expiration — trades open and close same day

### Implied Volatility Integration
- Support/resistance areas with elevated IV provide better credit
- IV spikes at key levels serve as market confirmation of level significance
- Speaker uses an IV overlay chart below the price chart

## Backtestable Parameters
- **Entry (Bullish):** SPX 30-min hammer candle at identified support level → next candle breaks above hammer high → sell put credit spread at the money, 5-point wide
- **Entry (Bearish):** SPX 5-min candle closes below support → sell call credit spread at the money, 5-point wide
- **Stop-loss:** Close position if SPX price moves 2-3 points beyond the confirming candle's extreme
- **Take profit:** Let expire (0DTE max profit if OTM at close)
- **Filters:** Only trade when IV is elevated at the support/resistance area; align with higher timeframe trend

## Actionable Takeaways
1. Build a pre-market game plan with support/resistance levels and trend direction before the market opens
2. Use the 30-minute timeframe as the primary decision chart, drop to 5-minute only for entry precision
3. Always require candlestick confirmation at areas of interest — never trade just because price touches a level
4. Set stops immediately after entry based on the confirming candle, not an arbitrary dollar amount
5. Start with small position sizes (1-2 contracts) and scale up as the framework becomes second nature
